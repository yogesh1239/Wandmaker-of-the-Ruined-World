Four years ago, the Gremlin Disaster stole electricity from the world.

It stole lives. It stole peace.

The biggest, worst disaster in human history had stolen so much, and it stole online shopping from me too.

It was an extremely serious, grave problem.

I couldn't browse the internet like before, click on a product that caught my eye, and buy it. It couldn't have been more inconvenient.

These days, product catalogs were just lines and lines of text on paper. They were dry, boring things without a single photo. No way they'd make anyone want to buy something.

That was going to be the sticking point when I pitched my magic wands to the mages from the Tohoku Hunting Association who were coming to help rebuild after the mushroom pandemic.

Just as the Blue Witch barely knew the Tohoku Hunting Association's mages, they barely knew her. Everyone in Tokyo knew the Blue Witch's name, but the moment you crossed a prefectural border, it faded.

It was hard to say whether my old strategy of using the strongest witch in the Tokyo Witches' Council as my poster girl to build name recognition would work on them.

I heard the mages in the aid delegation would go home in a week or so. I needed a fast, impactful, easy-to-understand way to sell them on my magic wands.

While I was wondering how to pitch them, the Blue Witch, who handled advertising and publicity for my magic wands, came up with a good idea.

Give them samples.

Written catalog specs wouldn't mean much to them.

Even telling them, “This is the brand the famous Blue Witch uses,” probably wouldn't mean much.

I'd give them magic-wand product samples and have them actually look at them, hold them, use them, and experience how amazing they were.

Then orders would definitely come pouring in. We have absolute confidence in our magic wands' performance and quality.

Early in the morning on the day the Tohoku Hunting Association's mages were coming to Tokyo, the Blue Witch came to Okutama to pick up the samples. When she found out the products still weren't finished, she started complaining.

“Listen. This is the kind of thing you make by the day before. Plan ahead better.”

“No, I want to improve the quality even a little more. The meeting isn't until afternoon. There's still time, right?”

“I'm the one who has to make up for your delay. Don't get cocky.”

“Sorry about that.”

When I apologized, the Blue Witch gave a resigned sigh and sat down in a chair at the edge of the workshop.

For all her complaints, she still waited for me. She's a good one. Sorry for always making unreasonable demands.

“But is there really that much to fuss over? The quality looks good enough.”

As she spoke, the Blue Witch picked up the general-purpose, mass-produced magic wand that had been lying on my workbench.

Well, yeah. I get what she's saying. That would probably go over well as a sample too.

Multilayer processing for massive magic amplification.

Spherical polishing.

Backlash-prevention mechanism.

With all three standard modifications for basic magic-wand functions, you can't really go wrong.

But this time, I wanted to go one step further. If I was putting out samples, I wanted them to be a new product. That would make them more of a talking point and draw attention.

“What I'm making now is a new type. Magic-school customization.”

“Magic-school customization?”

The Blue Witch parroted the words back to me. I stopped working, showed her the half-finished wand, and explained.

“Magic backlash happens because magic power flows back into the caster, right? Your freezing magic leaves the caster shivering, foresight magic wrecks the brain, eyeball magic causes blindness, and fire magic causes burns.”

“That's why the backlash-prevention mechanism reduces the backlash.”

“Right. So here's the extra twist. One more step. You know personal-color Gremlins? I'm using one in the backlash-prevention mechanism.”

Personal-color Gremlins were a type of melt-recast Gremlin made by mixing components extracted from blood into Gremlins melted at high temperatures. Once they cooled and hardened, the blood components dyed them the individual's own color.

If I used my blood, they were gold. If I used the Blue Witch's blood, they were blue.

With a hundred people, you'd get a hundred different Gremlin colors.

“These days, we only use personal-color Gremlins in amulets, but I kept running experiments to see whether they had other uses. Then I found a pretty useful property last night, so I'm hurrying to build it into the samples. I'll add it to Cyanos later too.”

“So what does all that mean? Spare me the theory. Keep it short.”

“In short, I found that personal-color Gremlin refined from your blood can reduce freezing magic's backlash even further because you're good at freezing magic. Compare them.”

When I handed her the half-finished wand, the Blue Witch gave me a dubious look, held the conventional wand in one hand and the new-type wand in the other, then cast magic.

“Freeze[ヴアアラー]. Freeze[ヴアアラー]... I see? The new type really does have slightly less backlash.”

“Right?”

After sending two bursts of cold mist from the wand tips, the Blue Witch nodded in understanding.

When the Blue Witch became a witch, she gained freezing magic. She was resistant to it too.

The backlash-prevention mechanism made with personal-color Gremlins refined from the Blue Witch's blood also had resistance to freezing magic. It could reduce the backlash from freezing magic even more.

“I haven't had many samples to work with, so I haven't done much testing, but I confirmed it with eyeball magic too. You know how casting eyeball-familiar magic makes your eyes squeeze tight and hurt? Using the Eyeball Witch's personal-color Gremlin reduces that backlash too.”

“Oh. Then what about fire magic and foresight magic?”

“We should see similar backlash reduction.”

“Are you a genius...? Leave things to Ori and technology jumps ahead a hundred years.”

“That's because I'm a genius Wand Maker.”

When I puffed out my chest, the Blue Witch laughed.

Bwahahaha! I'm a genius. Call me the ultimate, perfect form of “you get good at what you love.” I'll improve wands every chance I get.

Still, this new mechanism wasn't necessarily a complete upgrade over the old one.

For example, if I customized it to reduce the backlash from freezing magic, the backlash from magic other than freezing magic got worse.

If I customized it for eyeball magic, the backlash from magic other than eyeball magic got worse.

The backlash-reduction rate for one specific school of magic went up, but the backlash-reduction rate for other schools went down.

“There are downsides, sure, but you use almost nothing but freezing magic, right, Blue Witch? And I hear the Eyeball Witch uses almost nothing but eyeball magic. I think the benefits outweigh the downsides.”

“Right. Witches and mages find their own school of magic easiest to use and most stable. Other people's schools are hard to control, so they only use them as support. So this... magic-school customization? It should be well received. I'm grateful for it too.”

Magic-school customization for wands was basically attribute specialization. It had big benefits for Transcendents and wizards who fought with one school of magic as their mainstay.

For types who used all kinds of schools evenly, the downsides were probably bigger, but they could use whichever setup fit the situation.

The Blue Witch had given it her stamp of approval, so I confidently kept working on the samples' finishing touches.

I didn't know what kind of magic the Tohoku mages used, but whatever it was, there was no such thing as too much backlash reduction. I wanted them to enjoy the samples' custom options and learn how good Ori-made custom magic wands were.

The one I was making now was a wand customized for freezing magic, but of course I could do other magic-school customizations too. If customization is what you need, please place an order. Heh heh heh.

Eventually, I finished the custom wand too, and the samples were finally ready. In my special paulownia box packed with cushioning material, I carefully placed one general-purpose magic wand and one magic-school-customized wand.

I put in the specification sheets, closed the lid, attached a noshi marked “Gift,” and it was done.[^1]

“All right. I have a little time left. Might as well give Cyanos magic-school customization too.”

“If you can do it quickly, do it.”

“I can do it quickly. It won't even take ten minutes.”

“That really is quick.”

I took Cyanos from the Blue Witch and immediately got to work improving it.

Since the Blue Witch, a freezing-magic user, naturally used freezing magic a lot, I took the handle apart and replaced the default milky-white backlash-prevention mechanism inside it with one made from blue personal-color Gremlin. This further improved its reduction rate for freezing magic.

As I worked briskly, the Blue Witch watched with her cheek propped in one hand on the workbench, then spoke with awe.

“Every time I see it, your dexterity is on another level. It's practically magic.”

“It's normal to me, though.”

“What part of that is normal? You really aren't using magic, right?”

“I'm just naturally like this. It's my constitution. You know how there are short sleepers who are fine even with ridiculously little sleep? Or people who can wiggle their ears on command. I'm just another one of those freaks of nature.”

The Blue Witch still looked skeptical after I explained it. But there was no other way to explain it, so that couldn't be helped.

Humans are more amazing than they realize. There are people who survived being struck by lightning seven times, or people who got bitten by venomous snakes hundreds of times and ended up unaffected by venom that should normally be lethal. It's a true story that sounds fake.

Some people go beyond human limits. So is it really that strange for someone to have the manual dexterity of electronic precision machining equipment?

“You're static-prone too. You became a witch because of your constitution, right? Constitutions are amazing.”

“When you put it that way, I start to think so. Then is your tactlessness a constitution too?”

She said it sarcastically, so I gave a big nod and puffed out my chest.

“Of course it's a constitution. How do I put it? I was born without much interest in other people. Even pretending to be interested is a pain. My own analysis is that I don't understand the subtleties of people's emotions because I have no desire to know them. That's probably why people call me tactless.”

“You understand that much about yourself, and you still won't fix it...?”

“I'm telling you, it's a constitution. You can't handle spicy food, but would you train yourself until you could? Huh? You wouldn't, right? You get by just fine as long as you don't eat spicy food.”

I couldn't change my essential nature.

Both my dexterity and my poor social skills were part of who I was. Going against my essential nature would only make life hard. Grandpa had told me to live as myself in a place where I could live as myself.

“You're a pain. If you aren't interested in people, I bet you've never liked anyone either.”

“That's not true. I like the Blue Witch.”

“...”

I'd only said something completely obvious, but the Blue Witch quickly looked away and went quiet.

Huh? Why all of a sudden?

Why? Did I say something that upset her?

We're friends, so naturally I like her. In the first place, I wouldn't become friends with someone I didn't like.

If I were friends with someone I hated, that wouldn't be real friendship. It would be nothing more than playing at fake friendship.

Therefore, friends = liking the other person. The equation holds. It should.

There shouldn't have been any logical inconsistency, yet an unnatural interruption in the conversation had, in fact, occurred. That meant some profound mystery of communication beyond my understanding was probably at work.

“Why did you go quiet? Did I say something weird? If you tell me what's weird, I'll consider improving it.”

“...No. When I'm talking to you, sometimes I feel like I'm the one who's wrong. You have no tact or hesitation, but that no-guard, 100%-honest way you talk isn't something I hate. Stay as you are, Ori.”

“Uh. Okay...?”

I couldn't follow her logic, but apparently I was fine as I was. Well then, I guess I'm fine as is.

Even while we chatted away, my hands had never stopped working, so just as the conversation came to a break, I finished the improvements too.

The Blue Witch took the upgraded Cyanos Ver. 2.2 and the paulownia box for the Tohoku Hunting Association, then left. At a witch's speed, it would take no time at all to get from Okutama to the city center, where the meeting was being held.

If I could sell magic wands in Tohoku, my sales channels would expand greatly and I'd have more customers.

I had put everything I had into that product.

All that was left was for the Blue Witch, my Minister of Sales and Promotion, to do some proper selling.
