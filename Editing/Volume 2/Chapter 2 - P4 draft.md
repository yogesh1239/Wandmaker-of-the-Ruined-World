The first thing we heard was a high-pitched cry. The farther we went, the louder the cries became, and the more there were.

As we made our way through the snowy mountain, a troop of Japanese macaques appeared in front of us.

A troop of Japanese macaques sat on the branches of a huge, leafless tree covered in snow.

The hostile troop of Japanese macaques kept crying kee-kee as they threatened a huge creature below the tree.

“Hey, Ori. Look over there.”

“I see it. Shh, be quiet.”

We crouched low and whispered to each other as we watched the commotion, our hearts pounding.

The creature the troop of Japanese macaques was threatening really was Bigfoot.

It was a humanoid about 3 m tall. Its huge, shaggy body was covered in gray fur, and its squat frame had especially large feet. Its distinct animal stink hit my nose, and the Blue Witch pinched her nose shut.

It was there.

Bigfoot was really there!

...But that excitement only lasted a moment.

As I watched the legendary creature with bated breath, I started to see what had happened.

The troop of Japanese macaques seemed to want to drive Bigfoot away. They cried kee-kee and shook the branches, dumping snow onto Bigfoot.

Meanwhile, Bigfoot had gotten snow dumped over its head. It twisted its ugly monkey face into an even uglier shape and drooped its shoulders.

As it let out deep, grating growls and gently reached a huge arm up toward the branches, a mother monkey holding a baby screamed and clawed at it.

“Oloron,” Bigfoot roared sadly. A gray Gremlin glinted on the tip of its nose, telling us what Bigfoot really was.

“Ori. This is...”

“Yeah. It's a monkey that mutated into a monster.”

The owner of the footprints hadn't been Bigfoot.

It was a monkey that had mutated into a monster. It may have looked worthy of the name Bigfoot, but it wasn't a creature that had always looked like that.

Even though it no longer looked like its companions, it was still trying to return to their troop, only to be rejected.

S-sad. Was there even a monster this sad?

Don't do this to me. I'm the kind of guy who watches some sappy animal documentary and bawls his eyes out over it. Even if it was a hairy, stinky, huge monkey, seeing it look that sad made my chest hurt.

“...Maybe I'm still pretty lucky. Even though I'm no longer human, I can still live in human society.”

“Thaaat's heavy. That's got way too much painful experience behind it.”

The Blue Witch was hurting for a different reason than I was.

We'd come to look for a cute, fluffy white wolf, and this was one hell of a surprise. This wasn't what I wanted to see.

As my mood darkened, thick, gloomy clouds hung low overhead as if to pile on, and the snow finally started coming down hard.

Then the melancholy scene took a turn.

A white wolf emerged from the heavy snow.

The white wolf hurried to Bigfoot's side and rubbed its muzzle against Bigfoot's thick leg.

Then it planted itself proudly on all fours beside Bigfoot and barked fiercely at the openly hostile troop of monkeys crying kee-kee.

An angry roar echoed through the snowy mountains.

When the wolf barked louder than all of them put together, the monkeys immediately panicked and scattered.

The white wolf that had driven the monkeys away gave a victorious howl at the sky. A blue Gremlin, just as the Blue Witch had said, was embedded in its forehead.

Bigfoot, its shoulders still drooping, gave the white wolf a single stroke as it nestled close to offer comfort, then trudged off into the white landscape beyond the heavy snow.

The two shadows disappeared beyond the winter.

And so the monsters' little scene on the snowy mountain ended with a small measure of relief amid the sadness.

“I see. So monsters live in symbiosis too.”

“Huh?”

When I summed up the unusual thing we'd just seen and muttered in admiration, the Blue Witch made a sound like she'd heard an unfamiliar foreign language.

Huh? The Blue Witch didn't understand symbiosis? You used to be a high school girl. Did you even pay attention in biology class?

“The word symbiosis means living things of different species living together and affecting each other. When both sides benefit, that's mutualism. When only one side benefits, that's commensalism. When one side benefits while making the other suffer, that's parasitism. Going by what we just saw, the white wolf helped Bigfoot, so at least it wasn't parasitism, and—”

“Wait, wait, wait. I know what symbiosis is. That's not it. That's not what I mean. Why do you have to make things complicated like that? Those two were friends. That's all you need to know, right?”

“Huh?”

The Blue Witch cut me off, and this time it was my turn to make a sound like I'd heard an alien language.

Friends? That mutt and that ape? There's even a saying that dogs and monkeys don't get along. No way.

“Looking like you get along and actually getting along are two different things. Say you take a new hire along to introduce them to a client. Even if you think the new hire is useless trash, you'd put on a fake smile and pretend you got along, right? Would you look at that and think, Oh, this new hire and that person are good friends? No, right? Friendship is a pure relationship that exists beyond necessity, interests, and keeping up appearances. As long as I can't rule out the possibility that Bigfoot and the white wolf, living in the wild, were helping each other because necessity compelled them to raise each other's chances of survival, and were following a code of conduct that avoided upsetting the other and maximized their own benefit, it's premature to decide those two were friends.”

After I laid out my theory, the Blue Witch let out her biggest sigh of the day.

Come on. Had I said anything wrong?

“Ugh, fine, fine. I'll listen to your annoying friendship theory all you want. Now let's hurry down the mountain. The blizzard's setting in.”

The Blue Witch held my hand and led me down the mountain trail so we wouldn't get separated in the growing whiteout. Snow this heavy was rare even in Okutama.

We'd wound up witnessing some unexpected monster ecology, and I had plenty to say about that, but we'd also gotten to see the white wolf the Blue Witch had told me about.

Well, it wasn't a bad outing.
