We wandered along the snowy road beside the Tama River, but I saw fewer creatures than I'd expected. Well, it was winter, so that was only natural, but maybe because I had a ridiculously strong creature beside me, it felt like there were even fewer animals around than usual.

My breath drifted white in the air, while the Blue Witch's breath had no color. Small details like that reminded me that even if she looked human, she wasn't human after all.

After we'd walked for a while, the Blue Witch muttered.

“...This area really is peaceful. There are only weak monsters. Nothing tries to pick a fight when it sees me.”

“Yeah, true. I don't know if it's because of the area or what, but only monsters weak enough for Hendensho to chase off show up around here.”

When I waved my magic wand[Hendensho], the Blue Witch kept wandering along and tapped a branch of a deciduous tree bent under the weight of the snow with the tip of Cyanos, knocking a clump of snow onto the road.

“That's a good thing. It's better if there aren't any strong monsters.”

“But strong monsters give you good materials, right? We'd be in trouble if there weren't any.”

Monsters came in all levels of strength.

On one hand, there were super-weak slug monsters that were literally little more than slugs with hair and would die if you just stepped on them. On the other, there were disaster creatures like giant kaiju that would still cause casualties even if a bunch of ordinary Transcendents went after them together.

The Gremlins weak monsters had were small and poor quality.

The Gremlins strong monsters had were big and high quality.

To collect large Gremlins that made excellent core materials for wands, it was better to have strong monsters around.

When I offered my opinion as a Wand Maker, the Blue Witch threw the snowball in her hand straight into my face.

“Bwaa!?”

“Idiot. Strong monsters usually attack people. If you could get high-quality leather from them, would you allow a pack of man-eating tigers to prowl around your house?”

“W-Well, that'd be a little...”

“Right. It's true that you can get good materials from strong monsters, but it isn't worth it.”

According to the Blue Witch, who had lots of experience hunting monsters, there were plenty of problems with putting monster materials to use.

The problems with using monster materials could be divided into three major categories.

First, monsters were hard to defeat.

The monsters I'd fought to the death in Okutama with Hendensho in one hand counted as small fry. In central Tokyo, monsters many times stronger than the ones I'd struggled to chase off showed up all over the place.

Man-eating monsters popped up in urban areas like clockwork, so of course people died. At present, only witches and mages could defeat monsters above a certain level of strength.

Second, monster meat couldn't be eaten.

There were all kinds of monsters—chimera type, beast type, plant type, ghost type—but none of them were edible. They didn't taste bad, and some were even tasty, but eating them upset your stomach.

I knew that well because I'd tried to find some way to eat it and thrown up over and over. You couldn't eat monster meat, no matter whether you boiled it or grilled it.

On top of that, monsters tore up fields and crops just like wild boars and deer, which made them a pain.

You'd kill some obnoxious monster that had stuffed itself fat on vegetables you'd carefully raised, only to find you couldn't even turn it into food and take back the calories. They really pissed me off.

The exception was witches and mages, who could eat monster meat without any trouble.

Monsters also seemed fine eating each other according to the law of the jungle, so Transcendents, who had mutated from humans and become inhuman, were probably physiologically closer to monsters.

The final problem was material deterioration.

Monster materials were better than ordinary animal materials.

Their claws were sharp, their hides were tough, and their bones were sturdy.

You could even get materials with strange, magical properties you'd never expect from their appearance.

But those high-performance materials deteriorated over time. Magic power was the cause.

Monsters were magical by nature, so they had magic power in them.

Even after they died, magic power remained in their corpses, letting monster materials do things that seemed like magic.

But that also meant their special properties vanished if the magic power did.

All living things gradually lost magic power after they died. As they lost magic power, their performance deteriorated too.

Depending on the material, it lost its magic power and completely deteriorated in as little as a few days, or one month at most.

Bones as strong as steel became ordinary bones, and brilliantly glowing feathers lost their light and became ordinary feathers.

Even if you risked your life to defeat a monster, you couldn't eat its meat, and the remaining materials quickly deteriorated until they were useless. That wasn't worth it at all.

I got sick of listening to the Blue Witch go on and on about “Everything Wrong with Monster Materials!”

They were pests while they were alive, and killing them had no upside. Monsters were totally hopeless.

“So killing monsters doesn't do you any good...”

“That's right. But Ori changed things a little.”

“Me?”

Confused, I pointed at myself. The Blue Witch gave a little smile.

“Of all the materials you can get from monsters, Gremlins alone don't deteriorate over time. Gremlins were too difficult to process to be useful, but Ori can process them. Only Ori can process them. You can draw out their capabilities and turn them into magic wands.”

“Oh! No, you're right. That's right. That frozen giant kaiju you killed—once it finishes thawing, we can probably pull a fucking huge Gremlin out of it. Let's keep taking down strong monsters and grabbing huge Gremlins! We'll make tons of magic wands!”

“We're the ones risking our lives to fight the monsters for that.”

“...Sorry.”

In the end, it came back to that.

There were things to gain from monsters. But they weren't worth the risk.

That was the point.

For the sake of the people risking their lives to fight monsters, I had to make even higher-performance magic wands.

Make better wands.

Have them use those to defeat stronger monsters.

Get higher-quality Gremlins.

Use those Gremlins to make even better wands.

It'd be great if we could get a cycle like that going.
