As I wrapped up the conversation on my own and nodded to myself, the Blue Witch cleared her throat and spoke in a more serious tone.

"Uh, actually, I have one more thing."

"What?"

"Um, well... Cyanos... broke."

"Ugh."

Cyanos, which the Blue Witch held out looking guilty, was a spectacularly cracked-up mess.

I'd thought it looked like she was trying to hide Cyanos behind her back. So this was why?

"Sorry. I thought I was taking good care of it, but..."

"You really did a number on it. What did you do to make it like this?"

"I don't know. Great Glacier magic—uh, I fired the maximum-power wide-area freezing magic at maximum boost, and it ended up like this. I didn't hit anything with the wand or use it roughly."

When I examined the wand closely, I saw that the damage was concentrated in the wooden part. In other words, the handle.

"Hmm. Did the heavy recoil send you flying when you used magic?"

"No. There was magic backlash, but the physical recoil was weaker than a handgun's... Can you fix it?"

I nodded at the worried Blue Witch.

"Don't worry, I can fix it. The core is undamaged. If freezing magic did this, could it have been frost cracking?"

"Frost cracking?"

I couldn't help doing a double take at the Blue Witch tilting her head. Seriously? She used ice magic. She should at least know about frost cracking. It was her specialty.

The Blue Witch was a former high school girl who used freezing magic but knew nothing about freezing phenomena, so I reluctantly explained it to her.

"It's called frost cracking—written with the characters for 'freeze' and 'split.' It happens when wood gets to around -25°C or lower. In wood exposed to extreme cold, the moisture freezes and expands, splitting it wide open lengthwise. If you go into the snowy mountains of Hokkaido in the dead of winter, sometimes you'll hear something like a gunshot. That's frost cracking too—the sound of a tree splitting."

"I see. I do remember hearing a sound like that when I cast Great Glacier magic."

As I explained while showing her Cyanos's handle with its huge crack, the Blue Witch nodded as if remembering something.

"Then it was frost cracking after all. Wait in the living room. I'll fix it right away."

"I'm really sorry. Even though you spent so much time and effort making this wand for me, Ori..."

"Don't apologize for a flaw in the product! This failure is my responsibility as the craftsman!"

I snapped at the Blue Witch, who was getting dejected for the wrong reason.

I knew the Blue Witch used ice-type magic, but I hadn't even considered frost cracking. That was my fault.

Dammit. Humiliating. I'd thought I'd finished it perfectly before giving it to her.

At least it hadn't caused a product accident. The Blue Witch didn't look injured. If my product's defect had injured my best advertisement, I'd never stop regretting it.

The Blue Witch seemed guilty for the wrong reason. Rather than sit and wait quietly in the living room, she said she'd go wipe out every monster in Okutama.

Okutama was a peaceful land where strong monsters didn't show up for some reason, but even so, there were monsters as strong as hippos or giraffes. All I could do against monsters like that was drive them off; I couldn't kill them. If she was going monster hunting, that would actually help.

"Once I'm done clearing them out, I'll wait in the living room for the repair to be finished."

"Can you do that without Cyanos? Okutama's pretty big. The repair will definitely be done first."

"Don't underestimate me. I'm a witch, remember? I'll be done soon."

The Blue Witch set out looking confident, fully intending to wipe every monster out of Okutama before I finished my repair.

Cocky, wasn't she? But she shouldn't underestimate me, either. I was a dexterous man. Fixing a broken wand would take no time at all.

After seeing the Blue Witch off, I rolled up my sleeves and immediately got to work replacing Cyanos's handle in the workshop.

I removed Cyanos's handle, which had split badly lengthwise and become loose and shaky, then carved a new handle out of oven-dry wood with 0% moisture content. If the handle had split and broken because moisture froze and expanded, I just had to use wood dried to the absolute limit: oven-dry wood.

Oven-dry wood was like dry, rough skin that had lost all its moisture. It warped easily, cracked as it dried, and lost its good looks. I didn't like it much as a material. But if I needed to use oven-dry wood, then of course I'd use it.

Once I'd carved the new handle from the highest-quality heartwood I had, I checked how it fit with the core and made fine adjustments. Then I finished the surface with quick-drying varnish and treated it to keep it from absorbing moisture easily. Done. Only the material had changed; the basic design was the same as the handle I'd made last time, so the work went pretty quickly.

All of a magic wand's functions were in the core. Whether I made the handle carelessly or carefully had no effect at all on the magic's power or how easy it was to use.

Even so, my pride as a craftsman wouldn't let me do anything less than finish every detail properly. I was a seriously realistic craftsman.

Even if I did say so myself, it was fast, careful work. This time, I was sure the finish was perfect.

Just as I took Cyanos Ver. 2 to the living room, I heard footsteps from the hallway leading to the front door, and the Blue Witch walked in.

"What!?"

"So fast!?"

We came face-to-face, and both of us were shocked.

What? What was going on? Only about an hour had passed. Even if she'd said she was going to hunt the monsters in Okutama, it should've taken quite a while just to find them. There was no way she could've finished clearing them out so quickly.

"Did you just take a quick look around my house?"

"No. I cleaned all of Okutama. Here, proof."

With that, the Blue Witch emptied a clattering pile of Gremlins smeared with slightly fishy-smelling blood from her pouch onto the table. More and more came out of her coat pockets, breast pocket, and pants pockets too.

Eek. Was she for real? Did she hunt this many monsters in one hour? Witches were way more monstrous than I'd thought.

As I stood there both impressed and terrified, the Blue Witch gave Cyanos in my hand a suspicious look.

"Was that just a quick patch? With glue or something?"

"No. I remade the whole handle. Here, look."

When I handed Cyanos to her, the Blue Witch was surprised enough that I could tell even through her mask.

She ran her fingers along the handle, gave it a light swing, then stared at the rebuilt Cyanos in disbelief.

"I can't believe it. You made a new handle with this much fine decoration from scratch? By hand? It's only been about an hour."

"I'm dexterous."

"You're too dexterous..."

After spending a while half exasperated and half impressed, the Blue Witch thanked me properly, collected the Gremlins and Cyanos, and went home.

Hmm. I'd never seen how witches fought. If I got the Blue Witch's on-the-ground perspective, impressions from actual use, and feedback, I might find improvements I couldn't see from a craftsman's perspective.

I'd get her to let me do regular maintenance checks from now on.

I had to keep honing my skills to make better and better magic wands.
