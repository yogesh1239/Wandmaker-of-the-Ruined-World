## English Localization

- `4 — HIGASHIYAMATO CITY`
- `The territory of the Dragon Witch, the Tokyo Witches' Council's resident troublemaker. People elsewhere tend to assume that “any region ruled by that Dragon Witch can't possibly be decent,” but it is surprisingly well managed. Apart from being sent treasure hunting to satisfy the Dragon Witch's shameless desires, residents suffer little monster damage and have plenty of food. The crime rate is high, but it is all stupid petty crime. Strangely enough, serious crimes that threaten lives never happen. Perhaps residents take after their ruler.`
- `5 — BUNKYO WARD`
- `The territory of the long-suffering Foresight Mage. Minato Ward was once the center of Tokyo, but after the Bloodsucking Mage's death, that role sort of shifted over to Bunkyo Ward. Food rations are the bare minimum but nutritionally balanced. There is a barter market once a month. Crime is low, monster damage is rare, sensible reconstruction projects keep rolling out, and the ward offers fairness, freedom, and more. It is an extremely livable, energetic place. But do not forget that the Bunkyo Ward Office staff led by the Foresight Mage work themselves half to death to keep it that way.`
- `6 — MINATO WARD`
- `Formerly the Bloodsucking Mage's territory, it is now a burned-out ruin where no one lives. This unmanaged void in central Tokyo has become a breeding ground for monsters, causing damage in neighboring regions and creating a major headache. Former Minato residents want to reclaim their home from the monsters. But these times are not peaceful enough to retake land merely by speaking up, so for now they are only drawing up a recapture plan.`
- General notes: `Every district in Tokyo has a security force that protects people from monsters. Depending on the district, these groups are called defense forces, combat teams, armed police, volunteer corps, and other names. Their job is to hold out until a witch or mage arrives if the enemy is strong, or defeat it themselves if it is weak. They are armed with clubs, spears, crossbows, shields, and similar weapons. Firearms are precious because ammunition is almost gone. Household vegetable gardens are becoming more common in most regions. Even without a large field, people can grow a few vegetables in planters or flowerpots. It is an effort to improve the food situation, even if only a little. Composting to make fertilizer is also encouraged, but the stench is so awful that communities often collect human waste on the outskirts of town and make all their compost there.`
