## English Localization

- Heading: `VOLUME 1 SPECIAL EDITION AFTERWORD`
- Body: `I've written a few afterwords by now, and I'm plotting to write Japan's number-one afterword someday. Twice. An afterword written at Cape Soya, Japan's northernmost point, would be number one in Japan—the afterword written farthest north. An afterword written at the summit of Mount Fuji, Japan's highest peak, would also be number one—the afterword written at the highest place. The problem is that writing one is easy, but getting there is hard. Reaching Cape Soya requires a car or bus, and the bus is reservation-only. Didn't you have to book it a month in advance? There isn't even a full month between learning that I need to write an afterword and the submission deadline! Impossible! Mount Fuji also has a limited climbing season, and its trails are closed outside July through September. Besides, I'm not confident I have the stamina to reach the summit. Since around winter, I've been running in the middle of the night two or three times a week to build endurance, but I started out so weak that it is nowhere near enough. And so I still haven't written Japan's number-one afterword. But maybe these are just excuses? It does feel that way. If I set out on the trip before receiving the assignment, I would have enough time. Even with the climb, if I took my time and went up slowly, I probably wouldn't collapse halfway. Maybe I simply lack the passion and commitment to make Japan's number-one afterword happen. Am I secretly finding the whole thing a hassle? Well, not even secretly. It really is a huge hassle. Travel costs and time don't bother me, and I can make room for as much of either as needed. I just do not want to leave my house. Don't underestimate a homebody. If you someday open one of my books and find its afterword beginning with the claim, “This is Japan's number-one afterword,” please laugh and say, “They actually went and did it!” If you've read this far and thought, “What even is this afterword?” you are correct. I don't really know either. But I bet it made you chuckle a little. Wasn't it more interesting than an afterword that does nothing but describe the pain and hardship of writing and offer thanks to people you don't even know? Not that there's anything wrong with that. Still, if you've paid money for a novel—the special edition—you want the afterword at the end to be interesting too, right? When it's packed with fun all the way to the end, it feels like you got your money's worth. I'll be happy if you found this stupid afterword even a little entertaining.`
