## Verbatim Japanese

`ざっと資料に目を通し、三田は呟いた。「そっか。永堀くんの研究も佳境か」「はい、そう仰っていました……あの。永堀さんは大丈夫でしょうか」資料から顔を挙げると、慧は酷く不安げにしていた。三田は言葉に詰まる。大丈夫かどうかで言えば、恐らく大丈夫ではない。魔法言語の研究はとにかく死亡事故が多かった。魔法の詠唱を弄ろうとして不発に終わったなら幸運で、暴走したり、想定と異なる致死的な魔法が発動してしまったりした結果、既に四名が命を落としている。ほんの三日前にも一名が殉職したばかりである。慧は父に似て聡明だ。下手に「大丈夫だ」と気休めを言っても見抜かれる。なんと言えばいいのか悩んでいると、その沈黙から察したらしい慧は悲しげに目を伏せた。「三田さんは怖くないんですか？　私は、父が――」言葉を選ぶための間を一瞬だけ挟み、少女は慎重に言った。「――いなくなるのが怖いです」その言葉尻は細く震えていて、三田はたまらなく庇護欲が掻き立てられた。いくら賢くてもたった十一歳の女の子なのだ。命懸けで新たな学問と格闘する研究者たちの行動は認めがたいだろう。大人であっても否定的な者は多い。だが、三田の考えは違う。ここはその場しのぎの慰めではなく本気の言葉が必要だと感じた三田は、まだ平和だった頃を思い出しながらゆっくりと言った。「俺も死ぬのは怖いよ。それでもやりたいからやってる。慧ちゃんは、俺たちみたいな言語学者が普通の人に『言語学やってます』って自己紹介するとなんて言われるか知ってる？」「……質問でしょうか？　研究分野とか、研究内容とか」大真面目に嬉しい事を言ってくれる慧に微笑み、三田は首を横に振った。「その研究って一体なんの役に立つんですか？　だよ」心底困惑したような、あるいは半笑いで投げかけられる疑問を三田は忘れられない。それは「お前は役立たずだ」と言われているに等しい。もちろん、傷つけようとして言っているわけではない者が大多数だ。それでもその言葉は棘のように心に刺さって抜けず、折に触れて鈍い痛みに苛まれる。`

## English Localization

`Mita skimmed the pages, then murmured, “I see. Nagahori-kun's research is at the critical stage too.” “Yes, that is what Nagahori-san said... Um. Will Nagahori-san be all right?” When Mita looked up from the papers, Kei looked terribly anxious. He found himself at a loss for words. If the question was whether Nagahori would be all right, the answer was probably no. Magic-language research had produced an extraordinary number of fatal accidents. If an attempt to alter an incantation merely failed to activate, that counted as good fortune. Runaway magic and unexpected lethal spells had already killed four people. One researcher had died in the line of duty only three days ago. Kei was intelligent like her father. She would see through any careless reassurance that everything would be fine. While Mita struggled to decide what to say, Kei seemed to understand from his silence and lowered her eyes sadly. “Aren't you afraid, Mita-san? I'm afraid my father will—” She paused for only a moment to choose her words, then spoke carefully. “—disappear.” The end of her words trembled faintly, and Mita felt an overwhelming urge to protect her. However intelligent she might be, she was still only an eleven-year-old girl. It was only natural that she struggled to accept the actions of researchers risking their lives to grapple with a new field of study. Plenty of adults disapproved too. Mita, however, thought differently. This was not a time for makeshift comfort. She needed sincere words. Remembering the days when the world had still been peaceful, he spoke slowly. “I'm afraid of dying too. But I still do this because I want to. Kei-chan, do you know what ordinary people say when linguists like us introduce ourselves by saying, ‘I study linguistics’?” “...Do they ask questions? About your field or the subject of your research?” Mita smiled at her utterly serious, heartening answer, then shook his head. “They say, ‘What possible use is that research?’” Mita could not forget the question, whether it was tossed at him in genuine bewilderment or with a half-smile. It was the same as being told, “You're useless.” Of course, the vast majority of people did not mean to hurt him. Even so, those words lodged in his heart like a thorn and refused to come loose, leaving him with a dull ache whenever they came to mind.`
