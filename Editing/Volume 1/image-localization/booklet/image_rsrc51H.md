## English Localization

### 01 — And Then Civilization Fell

`A must-read for anyone who loves an outrageously slow-on-the-uptake protagonist who takes three weeks to notice that civilization has collapsed. If Ori had left Okutama at this point, he would have been swept into an astounding political drama of tangled human relationships, love, and hatred. His stomach would have died. He might literally have died too. Ori was lucky enough to find Okutameteorite after it fell into his backyard, and that was genuinely incredible luck. It should have used up a lifetime's worth, yet apparently his luck still has not run out. Amazing, protagonist.`

### 02 — Survival, with a Side of Magic

`Before electricity became widespread, people supposedly spent most of each day on basic work: drawing water, washing clothes, cooking, cleaning, shopping, and so on. Living in a world without electricity, gas, or running water is hard. In truth, a fair number of people tried leaving the noise of the city and surviving alone like Ori. Most of them died. Ori is one of those rare people who feels no stress from solitude. He can also make plans and follow through, he was lucky, and above all, he was dexterous. Over the course of a year, Ori trained himself and pushed ahead on his own path, building the foundation that would carry him through the events to come.`

### 03 — The Blue Witch

`The story in which Ori finally learns what has become of the world. When the Blue Witch found an intruder during her daily patrol of Oume City, she was fully prepared to break one or two of his limbs and throw him out. Many supplies remained in Oume, so a fair number of looters crept in after them. Even so, she questioned him first to make sure he was neither another witch's messenger nor an Oume resident whose face she had forgotten. The Blue Witch was dumbfounded that Ori was living so carefree in such brutal times. Ori, meanwhile, was terrified to learn that the world had become several times harsher than he had imagined.`

### 04 — Overtechnology

`The wand craftsman's proudest masterpiece makes its debut. He has created a monster of a weapon. In truth, the same object could be made if a state-of-the-art computer-controlled precision machine were operational. But making it by hand is the protagonist at his best, brute-forcing every problem with “Skill: Dexterity.” The world learns of a tool not of civilization, but of magic: the magic wand. Without a wand, insufficient firepower would make the giant kaiju impossible to defeat. It would freeze halfway, stagger back into the sea, then repeatedly return ashore and tear Tokyo to pieces.`

### 05 — Dragon-Furnace-Carved Seven-Layer Blue Wand Cyanos

`A story that deepens the relationship in which Ori Kenshi makes wands and the Blue Witch wields them. Magic wands can break and malfunction too. They require maintenance. Producing a one-hundred-percent finished product on the first attempt is extremely rare. The normal path is to learn from failures and use those lessons to improve the product. Listening to customers and improving the product is also part of a craftsman's work. The “dragon-furnace type” in Cyanos's formal name comes from a wild dragon. Ori discovered the multilayered structure of wand cores through a rabbit monster...`
