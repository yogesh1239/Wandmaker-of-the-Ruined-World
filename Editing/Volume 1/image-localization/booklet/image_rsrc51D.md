### 楠桜 — continuation

`両親がどこかで生きている事をずっと信じているが、残念ながら死んでいて、ゾンビの魔女が操るゾンビの群れに加わっている。扱いとしては行方不明。グレムリン災害で死体も確認されず、消息不明になった者は多い。楠桜もそうした行方不明者の家族を持つ、ありふれた悲劇を被った子供の一人だ。`

### 鹿屋辰巳（かのや　たつみ）

`元々小金井市に住んでいた。小金井市には元々魔女がいたが、災害から間もなくして頓死したため治安が崩壊しており、あちこちを点々として文京区に流れ着いた。溺愛していたペットのフェレット「ふーちゃん」を亡くしており、ペットロスで苦しみちょっとおかしくなっている。絶対音感と人間としてはズバ抜けた魔力を持ち、のちに東京魔法大学変異学科教授に就任する。動物しか愛せない。`

### 大利の絶縁

`大利は祖父に可愛がられていた。家族の中で祖父が一番大利に理解を示していた。祖父は大利の対人能力は改善すべき事と思っていたが、改善するために孫息子をどうこうしようとはしなかった。その祖父の葬式に、大利は出席しなかった。死体に会いに行っても意味が無いと思ったからである。実際には、大利は祖父が体調を崩した時にこまめにメールを送ったり果物を送ったりしていたし、実家暮らしで祖父が健康だった頃から肩たたきをしたり祖父の趣味の盆栽に付き合ったりしていた。「孝行したい時に親は無し」という言葉を真に受けて、祖父が死ぬ前に、痴呆が進み孫の顔が分からなくなる前に、まだ健康なうちに、孝行しようと考え実行していた。だが大利は自分の考えを口に出さなかったし、家族にとっては「可愛がってくれていた祖父の葬式に顔を出さないとんでもない奴」でしかない。それまでつもりつもっていた軋轢もあり、それが最後のトドメになって絶縁状態になった。一応、姉だけは大利の連絡先を知っている。だがその連絡もグレムリン災害によって不可能になった。`

### 文明遺産の活用

`冷蔵庫や車の車体は解体され、魔物に対する盾として活用されがち。標識のポールを棍棒にしたり、先端にナイフや包丁を縛り付けて槍にしたりもされる。使い道の無い部品は瓦礫と一緒に積み上げてバリケードになる。自転車は前時代の遺産の中でも特に重宝されている。数が多いし、移動用の乗り物としてそのまま活用でき、壊れてもチェーンやギアの部品をとって水車などに流用できる。人口の8割が死亡した結果、衣服が大量に余る事になった。焚きつけ、家屋の断熱材、包帯など、様々な用途に使用されている。もちろんそのまま着る事もある。スマートフォンはほんの短い間、通信に使えた事もある。使えないならいらな`

## English Localization

### Kusunoki Sakura — continued

`She continues to believe that her parents are alive somewhere. Unfortunately, they are dead and have joined the horde of zombies controlled by the Zombie Witch. Officially, they are treated as missing. Many people vanished in the Gremlin Disaster without their bodies ever being found. Kusunoki Sakura is one of the many children who suffered the commonplace tragedy of having missing family members.`

### Kanoya Tatsumi

`Originally a resident of Koganei City. Koganei once had a witch, but she died suddenly soon after the disaster, causing public order to collapse. After drifting from place to place, Kanoya reached Bunkyo Ward. He lost his beloved pet ferret, Fu-chan, and the grief has left him a little unhinged. He had perfect pitch and exceptional magic power for a human, and later became a professor in the Department of Mutation Studies at Tokyo Magic University. He can love only animals.`

### Ori's Estrangement

`Ori's grandfather doted on him and understood him better than anyone else in the family. His grandfather believed Ori should improve his interpersonal skills, but never tried to force his grandson to change. Ori did not attend his funeral because he saw no point in going to meet a corpse. In reality, when his grandfather's health deteriorated, Ori frequently emailed him and sent fruit. Even earlier, while living at home and while his grandfather was healthy, Ori massaged his shoulders and joined him in his bonsai hobby. Ori took literally the saying, “By the time you want to honor your parents, they are gone.” Before his grandfather died, before dementia advanced until he could no longer recognize his grandson, and while he was still healthy, Ori decided to show his devotion and acted on it. But Ori never explained his thinking. To his family, he was simply “the terrible bastard who refused to attend the funeral of the grandfather who adored him.” Combined with years of accumulated friction, that became the final blow and left him estranged from his family. His older sister alone still knows how to contact him, but the Gremlin Disaster made even that contact impossible.`

### Using the Legacy of Civilization

`Refrigerators and car bodies are often dismantled and used as shields against monsters. Signposts are turned into clubs, or utility knives and kitchen knives are tied to their ends to make spears. Parts with no other use are piled together with rubble to form barricades. Bicycles are especially prized among the relics of the previous age. They are plentiful and can be used as vehicles without modification. Even when broken, their chains and gears can be repurposed for waterwheels and other mechanisms. With eighty percent of the population dead, there is a vast surplus of clothing. It is used for kindling, home insulation, bandages, and many other purposes. Naturally, it is also still worn. Smartphones could be used for communication for only a brief time. Trades were made between people who thought, “If they don't work, we don't need them,” and...`
