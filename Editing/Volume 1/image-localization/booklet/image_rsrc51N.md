## English Localization

- Section heading: `SHORT STORY`
- Title: `FOUNDATION`
- Story: `Mita Ichiki had originally been an ordinary animal linguist, but a strange twist of fate brought him to live in Minato Ward. He now spent his days researching as a member of the magic-language research team led by Ohinata Kei. Mita was twenty-four years old, and at university he had studied eagle frogs. Eagle frogs were amphibians native to temperate alpine regions. In Europe and North America, they were also called parrot frogs because they mimicked the cries of birds of prey. A frog's natural enemy was the snake, while a snake's natural enemies included eagles, hawks, and other raptors. By imitating an eagle's cry, an eagle frog tricked a snake into thinking, “A predator's here! I have to get away!” and drove it off. The interesting part was that this mimicry was not innate. The frogs learned it after birth. Wild eagle frogs learned an eagle's cry by hearing one. In captivity, they could be taught the calls of sparrows, crows, pigeons, and other birds. Nor were they limited to birds. They mimicked cattle, pigs, and human voices, and one rare specimen had even imitated a doorbell chime. They truly were the parrots of the amphibian world. No other frog anywhere possessed such advanced vocal mimicry. Mita knew a great deal about eagle-frog mimicry and the many varieties of their calls. He had been investigating and documenting the frogs' unique, primitive language system. Then the Gremlin Disaster struck in the middle of his research. His work was suspended indefinitely, and even Mita's survival became uncertain. Ohinata Laboratory took him in. Magic language was entirely unknown. Since it even contained phonemes humans could not pronounce, Ohinata Laboratory expanded its comparative research to nonhuman subjects. If humans could not pronounce the language, perhaps studying the language systems of nonhuman creatures would help decipher it. As a researcher of the eagle frog's language system, Mita was welcomed into Ohinata Laboratory to investigate that possibility. Tonight, once again, Mita was holed up in a corner of the laboratory. Sandwiched between towering stacks of reference books, he sat on a book in place of a chair and sharpened a pencil with a knife while squinting in the lantern light.`
