# Character Voices — Wand Maker of the Ruined World

## Narrator
- **Register ceiling:** casual, punchy past-tense narration; contemporary and plain, never more literary or formal than the source passage.
- **Mode:** mixed: Ori's chapters use first-person narration, while alternate chapters and sections use close third-person with rotating focal characters.
- **Active-viewpoint guide:** Identify the viewpoint from the chapter opening, first-person self-reference, named focal character, territory/work, and limits of knowledge. Ori's first-person narration is anxious, snarky, craft-obsessed, and full of abrupt comic reactions. Close-third passages stay in the narrator's plain register while tracking the focal character's knowledge and emotional emphasis; do not turn them into first-person character narration.

## Ori Kenshi
- **Pronoun:** 俺 (*ore*) — blunt, casual masculine self-reference; his English is plain, impulsive, and self-important when craft is involved.
- **Register:** very casual. Use contractions, fragments, rhetorical questions, and fast exclamations; let panic turn into stammers and repeated words.
- **Address / honorifics:** initially says "Blue Witch-san," then normally "Blue Witch" without an honorific; after the explicit change in Volume 3, he calls her "Hiyori." He consistently calls Ohinata "Professor Ohinata" and uses titles such as "Dragon Witch." Preserve each source shift rather than normalizing it.
- **Tics / particles:** rapid self-questioning, repetitions, stammers, sentence fragments, and blunt interjections. His emphatic reactions and hard stops are comic beats, not polished prose; choose the English interjection line by line rather than assigning him a fixed filler.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** talks like a shut-in hobbyist: online selling, anime, tools, product specs, and overblown comparisons. He is evasive and small-voiced around people, but becomes technical, fluent, and smug about magic wand work; fear produces frantic internal commentary rather than elegant reflection.
- **Samples:**
  - 俺は世界一の魔法杖職人[ワンドメーカー]だ。 → "I'm the world's greatest Wand Maker." (*Vol. 2, “The White Fingers”*)
  - 「うわっ！　い、いいからそれ持ってあっち行け！　失せろ話しかけんな！　しっしっ！」 → "Whoa! J-Just take that and go! Get lost! Don't talk to me! Shoo!" (*Vol. 1, “Survival, with a Side of Magic”*)
  - 「これが俺が作った足踏み動力式の研磨機。こうやって椅子に座って、足元のクランクペダルを踏むだろ？」 → "This is the pedal-powered grinder I built. You sit here and step on the crank pedal at your feet, see?" (*Vol. 2, “The White Fingers”*)

## Aoyama Hiyori / Blue Witch
- **Pronoun:** 私 (*watashi*) — controlled and neutral; English should stay spare and firm rather than feminine-coded.
- **Register:** plain, terse, and commanding. She rarely wastes words; complete sentences appear when she explains magic or takes responsibility.
- **Address / honorifics:** calls him "Ori" in direct speech and Ohinata "Kei-chan." Others are usually titles, including "Foresight Mage" and "Eyeball Witch." Retain any source honorific shift.
- **Tics / particles:** short commands, flat declaratives, and occasional rough sentence endings. Hesitation appears as pauses or broken starts when she is embarrassed; do not add habitual sighs, snark, or fillers not present in the line.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** practical combat, patrol, territory, magic power, and duty. Her cold baseline cracks into blunt care for people from Ome, grief over those she lost, and rare awkward hesitation; keep those turns quiet.
- **Samples:**
  - 「待て」 → "Wait." (*Vol. 1, “The Blue Witch”*)
  - 「お前のはほとんど最弱の魔法だからな？　調子に乗るなよ」 → "Your magic is practically the weakest there is, got it? Don't get cocky." (*Vol. 1, “Life with a Witch”*)
  - 「私は魔女だからな」 → "I'm a witch, after all." (*Vol. 1, “The Blue Witch”*)

## Ohinata Kei
- **Pronoun:** 私 (*watashi*) — standard but youthful; English sounds bright, careful, and unusually well organized for her age.
- **Register:** polite and eager. Use full sentences and polite enthusiasm without making her babyish; academic explanations stay clear and methodical.
- **Address / honorifics:** "Ori-san" and "Foresight-san" are stable forms. She initially says "Blue Witch-san" in formal reference but calls Aoyama "Ao-san" once they are close; preserve the form used at that point in the source. Keep her respectful honorifics.
- **Tics / particles:** energetic greetings, small self-corrections, and frequent exclamation marks during excitement. She can launch into a prepared lecture rhythm; do not force a recurring English interjection where the source has none.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** magic-language analysis, research method, phonetics, and teaching. Her happy, chatty curiosity drops into simple, steady resolve when discussing her father, dead colleagues, or dangerous research.
- **Samples:**
  - 「こんにちは、初めまして。大日向慧です！」 → "Hello, it's nice to meet you. I'm Ohinata Kei!" (*Vol. 1, “Magic Linguistics”*)
  - 「では失礼して。それでは講義を始めさせてもらいますね。あ、トイレとか大丈夫ですか？」 → "If you'll excuse me, I'll begin the lecture. Oh, do you need to use the bathroom first?" (*Vol. 1, “Magic Linguistics”*)
  - 「私は自分の手で父の研究を完成させたいんです」 → "I want to finish my father's research with my own hands." (*Vol. 1, “Magic Linguistics”*)

## Foresight Mage
- **Pronoun:** 俺 (*ore*) — a weary former office worker's casual self-reference; English is gruff but not macho.
- **Register:** mostly plain and conversational, with fast, data-heavy speech when explaining policy. He uses dry sarcasm and sounds exhausted even when being helpful.
- **Address / honorifics:** uses titles such as "Blue Witch" and "Flame Witch-sama"; calls Ohinata "Ohinata-kun" and the Bloodsucking Mage "Bloodsucking." Keep source forms.
- **Tics / particles:** dry sarcasm, weary pauses, and a recurring "unfortunately" used with bitter humor. Under severe magic backlash he switches from 俺 (*ore*) to 僕 (*boku*) and regresses into slurred, childlike simplicity; preserve the abrupt contrast without adding baby talk beyond the source.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** food supply, logistics, projections, numbers, administration, and future consequences. His fatigue turns into an urgent flood of facts when lives are at risk; privately, he craves praise, dreams of retirement farming, and talks himself down rather than becoming melodramatic.
- **Samples:**
  - 「いる。残念ながら」 → "I'm here. Unfortunately." (*Vol. 1, “Tokyo Witches' Council”*)
  - 「パワーが要るんだ。火力でもいい、マンパワーでもいい、未知の新エネルギーでもいい、なんでもいい。」 → "We need power. Firepower, manpower, some unknown new energy source—anything." (*Vol. 1, “Tokyo Witches' Council”*)
  - 「東京近郊だけで３００万人以上が餓死するクソったれの未来を変えられるんだ」 → "We can change that shitty future where over three million people starve to death around Tokyo alone." (*Vol. 1, “Tokyo Witches' Council”*)

## Eyeball Witch
- **Pronoun:** 私 (*watashi*) — an adult woman's neutral self-reference; her English is warm, tactful, and gently managerial.
- **Register:** polite-casual, with soft requests and conciliatory phrasing. She avoids making a hard command unless an emergency forces it.
- **Address / honorifics:** affectionately calls Aoyama "Ao-chan" and respects guests with names plus honorifics, such as "Okyaku-san." Retain the nickname and honorifics exactly; they signal familiarity and tact.
- **Tics / particles:** sentence-softening feminine endings, friendly invitations, and requests instead of orders. Naturalize them by context rather than repeatedly inserting "you know" or "please." Her anxiety shows in quicker, more direct phrasing, not a sudden formal register.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** meetings, coordination, supplies, and keeping the witches talking. Normally nurturing and roundabout; in a crisis, she gets to the point while still asking rather than ordering.
- **Samples:**
  - 「緊急事態よ」 → "It's an emergency." (*Vol. 1, “Overtechnology”*)
  - 「ねえ青ちゃん、こっちに来てくれない？　お願いよ、一人でも戦力が欲しいの！」 → "Ao-chan, could you come over here? Please. We need every fighter we can get!" (*Vol. 1, “Overtechnology”*)
  - 「何も話さなくていいから、会議の話を聞いてくれてるだけでも良いのよ？」 → "You don't have to say anything. Couldn't you at least listen to the meeting?" (*Vol. 1, “Tokyo Witches' Council”*)

## Dragon Witch
- **Pronoun:** 私 (*watashi*) — self-reference remains adult, while her transformed speech pattern gives the English its childish, possessive flavor.
- **Register:** blunt, selfish, and playful-dangerous. Keep sentences short and declarative. Her recurring sentence-ending の (*no*) is a transformed speech trait, not a word to transliterate; convey its childish insistence through syntax and emphasis without appending "-no."
- **Address / honorifics:** calls Ori "Ori" and speaks of other witches by their titles. She does not use honorific politeness as a default.
- **Tics / particles:** repeated sentence-ending の, possessive "mine," abrupt questions, and imperative commands. Her laughs are loud and shameless; do not sanitize threats into teasing or mechanically reproduce の in English.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** treasure, shiny things, her nest, food, and ownership. She is delighted and greedy around valuables, then instantly frightening when crossed; keep both moods in the same casual voice.
- **Samples:**
  - 「うーん…………分かったの。決めたの。お前、私の巣でお宝作るの」 → "Hmm... Fine. I've decided. You're going to make treasure in my nest." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)
  - 「脱走禁止なの。えーっと、確かこのへんに……あったの。ほら、手錠なの。」 → "No escaping. Let's see, they should be around here... Found them. Here, handcuffs." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)
  - 「しつこいの。これは私の物なの。こんなまんまるピカピカでおっきい魔石、お前にはもったいないの」 → "You're so annoying. This is mine. A big, round, shiny magic stone like this is wasted on you." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)

## Zaizen Kintaro
- **Pronoun:** 私 (*watashi*) — deferential adult male self-reference; English should be courteous, measured, and lightly bureaucratic.
- **Register:** consistently polite. Use complete sentences, gentle hedges, and apologies; never turn his caution into comic cowardice.
- **Address / honorifics:** calls the Dragon Witch "Dragon Witch-sama," Aoyama "Blue Witch-sama," and Ori "Ori-san." Retain these forms.
- **Tics / particles:** elongated hesitations, cautious explanatory pauses, and sentence-softening polite endings. He softens bad news before delivering it; do not add stock phrases such as "if you please" without source support.
- **Vocabulary / contrast:** administration, work assignments, supplies, district conditions, and practical compromises. He remains civil under pressure; unease appears as word choice and hedging, not open defiance.
- **Samples:**
  - 「あ～、ちょっと難しいですねぇ。私、財前金太郎と申します。竜の魔女様の縄張りの事務担当のような事をやらせていただいています」 → "Ah, that would be a little difficult. My name is Zaizen Kintaro. I handle something like the administrative work in Dragon Witch-sama's territory." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)
  - 「まあ、住めば都と言いますし」 → "Well, as they say, home is where you make it." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)
  - 「大利さんも何かありましたら、まず私に話を通して下さい。竜の魔女様は……ちょっとアレなので」 → "Ori-san, if anything comes up, please speak to me first. Dragon Witch-sama is... a little difficult." (*Vol. 1, “The Dragon Witch”*)
