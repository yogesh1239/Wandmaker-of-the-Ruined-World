Three months had passed since the giant kaiju freezing incident.

I was still living as a shut-in in Okutama. Thanks to the Blue Witch's diligent food and supply deliveries, I went hunting far less often and had more time to tend my rice paddy instead.

There were still canned goods and preserved food left from before civilization collapsed. Ironically, because the population had dropped so much, consumption was down, and the food left in the cities hadn't been eaten up.

But they'll run out someday. Securing a stable food supply is a major challenge.

If this rice paddy works out, it'll at least solve my own food problem. I've had enough of year-old and two-year-old rice. I want fresh rice.

I'd found some chickens living wild in the mountains after escaping from who-knew-where, and I tried raising them, but they escaped again. So that failed. When I found a feather stuck to the top of my 2-meter fence, I despaired.

They'd looked like their wings were too stunted for them to fly, but the bastards flew off anyway! I was tricked! Damn them!

Some things had worked out, though.

My fishing methods have improved a lot. Before, I fished with a rod or set traps; now, I've learned to catch fish with magic.

When I spot a big fish silhouette, I blast it with a magic beam. Then it floats up stunned, and I scoop it up in a net. That's it.

It's simple, but effective.

According to the Blue Witch, I apparently had quite a lot of magic power. It was about 1/200 to 1/250 of the Blue Witch's.

After the Gremlin Disaster that collapsed human civilization, every human had come to possess at least some magic power.

One theory holds that magic power has always existed and has merely become observable, but the leading theory is that people gain it when Gremlins react to bioelectric current.

Every human has a bioelectric current. It's an extremely tiny amount of electricity, but there is a clear link between the amount of bioelectricity and the amount of magic power.

Apparently, an electrically charged constitution like the Blue Witch's generates huge amounts of magic power and mutates its owner into a Transcendent. The trade-off is that Gremlins crystallize inside the body and destroy it from within. I'm lucky to have only a moderate amount of magic power.

I can see the appeal of having magic power on a witch's level, but not if getting it means risking my life.

Anyway.

I spent my days farming and learning about the world that had turned fantastical.

Then one day, the Blue Witch came over for Cyanos's regular maintenance. I asked her about a monster that had moved into the wild wasabi patch along the stream, and she readily agreed to kill it.

She'd taken down a giant kaiju bigger than a building when it came ashore in Tokyo, so some small-fry monster causing pest damage out in the country probably isn't a problem for her.

Even so, she didn't hesitate. It was probably because she was strong, but she approached combat differently too. Three months ago, she'd wiped out Okutama's monsters at insane speed, and it was obvious she'd gotten completely used to fighting for her life—something that should've been alien to a modern Japanese person.

The difference in power between the Blue Witch and me was as vast as heaven and earth, but we still did the same thing: wielded wands, chanted incantations, and used magic. Thinking I might learn something, I asked if I could come along and watch her hunt the monster. Once again, the Blue Witch agreed without much thought.

"I'll look out for you too, but protect yourself as much as you can."

"Don't underestimate me. I can use magic too. I can at least defend myself."

"Your magic is practically the weakest there is, got it? Don't get cocky."

The Blue Witch had Cyanos, I had Hendensho, and we climbed the mountain path along the stream, chatting as we went.

When it hadn't rained for a while, the little stream dwindled until it disappeared beneath mossy rocks and undergrowth. The deafening chorus of midsummer cicadas drowned out both the rustling forest and the sound of the stream, forcing the same old hot, muggy summer feeling from before the Gremlin Disaster on me until I'd had enough.

But the Blue Witch was apparently using some kind of magic, because being near her felt nice and cool.

Just what you'd expect from an ice-type witch. I want to keep her around all summer in place of my dead AC-kun.

After walking along the stream for about ten minutes, we reached its source. Clear spring water seeped from a crack in a small cliff and made a little pond, where bright green wasabi plants spread their leaves.

The days when you could buy tubes of wasabi online are long gone. Now this wild wasabi patch is a source of a rare spice. I'm not about to let a monster take it from me.

"Not here? No, it's hiding somewhere."

The monster occupying this place looked like a wild-dog-sized turtle with an abnormally developed tail. The last time I'd seen it, it had been sunning itself by the water. The instant it spotted me, it let out a shriek and tried to control the pond water and slam it into me. Even when I fired magic at it, its tough shell held up. It was a pain I couldn't beat.

No matter how many monsters you kill or drive off, more keep popping up. Ordinary animals can mutate into monsters overnight, so they're a real pain. Even so, killing one takes care of the immediate problem.

I searched for the monster that had to be lurking somewhere, wand ready as I carefully watched the area.

But the Blue Witch just stood there holding Cyanos. She didn't look tense in the slightest.

"Ori, it's up in that tree. It's watching us from the base of the thickest branch."

"Huh!?"

When I looked where the Blue Witch casually pointed with her chin, the turtle monster was indeed there, its shell's bumps and colors expertly blended into the bark as camouflage.

Seriously!? How'd she spot that thing?

"I'm the one killing it, right?"

"Yeah. But seriously, how did you find it? I thought I was pretty good at spotting creatures in the forest too."

"I can sense magic power. That monster hid its form, not its magic power."

After briefly explaining how she'd detected it, the Blue Witch readied Cyanos. Apparently, she was ready to go.

Very well, then. Let's see it, shall we? Blue Witch-sama in magical combat, the same woman said to have slaughtered a giant kaiju in one blow.

As I watched with bated breath, the Blue Witch kicked hard off the ground and leaped nearly 10 meters in an instant.

Carrying the momentum of her jump, she spun and landed a vicious somersault kick on the turtle monster crouched motionless on the branch.

Her boot sliced through the air. The turtle monster burst into tiny pieces like a balloon popping.

The monster had made an ordinary Wand Maker throw in the towel, and the Blue Witch had just killed it with one kick. She landed lightly on the leaf mold piled on the ground and spoke curtly to the dumbstruck me.

"It's over."

"O-Oh. Thanks. You didn't use magic, even though you're a witch...?"

"Didn't need to. Spending magic power on a monster I could handle by jumping and kicking would be a waste."

"Y-Yeah."

Since she was a woman called a witch, somewhere in the back of my mind I'd expected her to show me a fantasy battle where she wielded a magic wand with style and fought smart.

But this is what I've gotten.

It's smart not to waste magic power, but I still can't quite accept it.

I mean, that is some serious meathead brute force. Is Blue Witch-sama perhaps a forest sage[^1]?

"What's with that face? I killed the monster like you asked."

"No, you helped. Seriously. As thanks, I'll make you some char pickled in wasabi soy sauce next time. But, see, I'd been hoping to learn something about fighting with magic. What you showed me was totally useless for that, so it was kind of a letdown."

When I told her how I really felt, the Blue Witch seemed to get it.

"Oh. If that's what you wanted, you should've said so sooner. Your noisy magic that sounds like a screaming beaver is almost useless in a real fight. Want me to teach you some new magic? Something even you might be able to use."

"Seriously? Please do. Then put together a concise report of about three A4 pages on practical magic even I can use and submit it to me."

"Looks like before I teach you magic, I need to teach you how to behave when someone's teaching you something..."

I tried to steer us toward communicating in writing by explaining how useful it would be to reread later or pass along to other people, but the Blue Witch couldn't be bothered to write and organize it all.

Why do people insist on getting things done face-to-face? I don't get it, but the person teaching says an oral lesson is easier, so it can't be helped.

I didn't want to annoy the Blue Witch and have her stop teaching me magic, so the moment we came down the mountain, I set up a target for shooting practice in the backyard and obediently took my teacher's lesson.

The Blue Witch was apparently going to teach me the most basic freezing spell, "Freeze[^2]," and an attack spell derived from it, "Freezing Javelin[^3]."

I readied Hendensho while the Blue Witch used her bare hands, and we each aimed at a target drawn on cardboard and hung from a tree branch.

"Vaaah-ra!"

"Wrong. Vaa-ra. Don't draw out the 'Va.' Pronounce 'Va,' then a separate 'a.'"

"Vaa-ra?"

"Closer. Now it's the pitch. The best way is to listen and learn—Vaa-ra!"

When the Blue Witch crisply demonstrated the incantation, a pale-blue freezing beam shot from her raised palm and struck the target. It froze in an instant, coated in a thin layer of ice and giving off white mist.

When I clapped in admiration, the Blue Witch gave a little hmph.

"This is as basic as it gets. Come on, pronunciation practice. Repeat after me: Vaa-ra."

"Vaa-ra."

"Again. Vaa-ra."

"Vaa-ra. This kind of reminds me of English class. It feels more like an actual language than an incantation."

"Of course. It's magic language."

"It's an actual language?"

My interest piqued, I asked another question. The Blue Witch noticed I'd lost focus, said, "Let's take a little break," and sat on a log. I sat on a stump opposite her.

Practical training was on hold. It was time for a theory lecture.

"I'm not an expert either, but according to linguists who analyze incantations, it's a proper language with its own grammar and context."

"Huh. So where'd you learn magic language, Blue Witch?"

"Every witch and mage—in other words, every human who physically mutates into a Transcendent—wakes from the resulting coma already knowing magic suited to them. It's intuitive, like you'd somehow learned to swim or ride a bicycle without realizing it... It's hard to explain."

"No, I kind of get it."

Something acquired later in life, learned by feel, and impossible to forget once you knew it. That was probably what she meant.

"I don't know why incantations get etched into our heads, but monsters also seem able to use magic suited to them right after they mutate. I think it's probably the same principle."

"Then does that mean monsters know magic language from birth?"

"It's the other way around. Magic language incorporates the sounds monsters make. For example, the Vaa-ra we're practicing now means 'Freeze,' but according to linguists, meanings are assigned to specific phonetic symbols."

"...?"

"In other words, when you pronounce 'Vaa-ra,' things freeze. So they go, 'Let's define this pronunciation as meaning Freeze and incorporate it into the language system.' It seems the system of magic language was built in that order."

"Um, is that like translating the scream 'Eek!' when you're scared as 'I'm scared!'?"

"That's mostly right. Probably."

The Blue Witch nodded vaguely.

She didn't sound like she knew much about it herself. Then again, she'd said it was intuitive.

"Transcendents can control magic power, so even if they pronounce an incantation correctly, they can keep magic from going off by accident. But you're not a mage, Ori. If you pronounce an incantation correctly with a Gremlin or magic stone nearby, the magic will activate on its own even if you don't mean it to, so be careful."

"Huh. That'd be a problem if I accidentally said an incantation mid-conversation."

"Magic-language pronunciation is completely different from Japanese. That won't happen. Also, magic language contains at least seven sounds that only a Transcendent's specially mutated throat can produce. They're especially common in advanced magic."

"Sounds I can't pronounce? Those exist? I kinda want to hear one."

"Let's see... Let moonlight and cool breezes alike all become ice[^4]."

The Blue Witch chanted lightly, like a chirping bird, and put her palm on the log she was sitting on. The log kept its shape but transformed entirely into ice.

Seriously? Holy crap! That isn't alchemy—it's ice-chemy!

"Ah. Sorry, was this a log for firewood?"

"No, it's fine, it's fine. Don't worry about it. That's amazing!"

Now that's something worth seeing. When I gave her a big round of applause, she looked a little proud this time.

So that's it. Witch-only incantations, huh? Lucky.

But I have an original incantation—or rather, an original scream—too.

"Does my Okutameteorite natural frequency incantation mean something in magic linguistics too?"

"Natural... what?"

"My magic that shoots a white beam. The one with the incantation that sounds like a screaming beaver."

"That? I don't know. Judging by the incantation and effect, I'd say it's close to the Northern Witch's shooting magic. But like I said, everything I just told you was secondhand. I'm not an expert on magic language myself. I only know a few incantations."

"I see..."

"If you're that interested, want me to get you some materials from someone who researches incantations?"

"Seriously? Please do."

I nodded at the Blue Witch's suggestion without a second thought.

Knowledge of magic language is bound to be good inspiration for making magic wands.

"All right. It'll be after the next Witches' Council meeting before I can bring it, so... wait five days."

Score! I can't wait for five days from now!

## Translator Notes

[^1]: The source writes “forest sage” (森の賢者) but gives it the unexpected reading “gorilla,” making Ori's grand description a gorilla joke.

[^2]: The written spell name means “Freeze,” while its spoken magic-language form is `Vaa-ra`; the pronunciation lesson distinguishes it from Ori's mistaken elongated version.

[^3]: The source writes “Freezing Javelin” but gives its spoken incantation as `ドウ・ヴアアラー`.

[^4]: The source gives the spoken form as `××・××フイフイ・イイヴアアラー`; the `××` marks sounds ordinary humans cannot pronounce. The English renders the written meaning without supplying those redacted sounds.
