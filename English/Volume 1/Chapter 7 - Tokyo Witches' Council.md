"I'm glad you're joining us, Ao-chan. I wish I could've served you some snacks with tea. I'll send you some next time."

"No need. I'm the one who said I wanted to join remotely."

On the day of the Witches' Council meeting, the Blue Witch attended from home through one of the Eyeball Witch's familiars.

The gathering of witches who controlled the heart of Japan, now called the Tokyo Witches' Council, had originally been a loose group formed amid the chaos right after the Gremlin Disaster.

The Eyeball Witch had started it. To learn what was happening in the world and what had happened to her, she created a place where people in similar situations could regularly exchange information.

Once the Bloodsucking Mage joined, it took on a stronger political character. They divided Tokyo into districts and clearly defined each witch's and mage's territory. By redistributing them evenly, they kept the strongest people—the ones capable of maintaining public safety—from becoming concentrated in one place, greatly improving safety across Tokyo.

They had also created a system for requesting help from other witches when badly matched monsters appeared, set up exchanges for supplies their districts lacked, such as food, medicine, and fuel, assigned refugees flowing in from Saitama and Chiba, gathered engineers and medical workers, and made plans to preserve and use their technical expertise. They had made many useful political decisions so far.

When the Iruma Mage staged his coup, they had put it down despite heavy losses and succeeded in turning that into public support.

But most witches and mages knew little about politics.

They were only ordinary people who had gained power. They could handle the simple, straightforward job of killing monsters with magic, but politics built on complicated conflicts of interest was hard.

Once the Bloodsucking Mage, who had led and kept everyone in line, died and left the stage, attendance at the Witches' Council dropped sharply.

Clashes and friction between witches increased, and several projects already underway stopped or were postponed.

At its peak, the Witches' Council had gathered 6 mages and 20 witches. Now it had dropped to 1 mage and 18 witches.

Only 6 of them showed up for regular meetings.

After the Bloodsucking Mage died, the Eyeball Witch became the Council's coordinator. Put kindly, she valued harmony. Put badly, she lacked decisiveness.

She knew it herself. She could handle a role like PTA president in peaceful times, but coordinating a Witches' Council that could decide Tokyo's fate was too much for her.

But nobody suited the role better than the Eyeball Witch. All she could do was keep the Council meeting regularly, maintain cooperation even if only in form, and at least keep things from getting worse. That was all she could manage.

Since the Blue Witch never left Ome, the only time she had regularly attended the Witches' Council in Shinjuku was during its early days.

This was the first time she had attended remotely since the emergency meeting held in Akiruno right after the giant kaiju invasion incident.

"I'm not joining your political playacting. Once I'm done with Foresight, I'm leaving."

"Is that so? You don't have to say anything. Couldn't you at least listen to the meeting? I'm sure you'll hear something useful for you too, Ao-chan."

"I'm fine. Foresight, are you there?"

When the Blue Witch called out, there was a pause. Then someone quietly snapped, "Answer! The Blue Witch is asking you!" After that, a weary middle-aged man's voice answered.

"I'm here. Unfortunately."

"I only have one thing to ask. It'll be quick. You were protecting a magic language researcher, weren't you?"

"...Are you planning to kill them?"

"Why would I kill them? I'm not an indiscriminate murderer. Lend me the materials they have. The magic language ones."

"Magic language research hasn't gotten anywhere yet. What would you even use the materials for?"

"What else would I use them for besides studying magic language?"

"Hah. You never showed even a shred of interest before, so that's suspicious! I have no intention of going against Blue Witch-sama's request, but lending you the materials would be difficult. Unfortunately."

"Why?"

"There's only one complete set of the original research materials. The copy machine doesn't work. It'd be a problem if I lent them out and they got lost."

The Foresight Mage's words made sense. They were confidential documents that could not be copied. It would be stranger to lend them out so easily.

But the Blue Witch would be in trouble if he refused to lend them to her. She had promised to bring magic language materials to Ori.

"You don't have to worry about Ao-chan. She always returns what she borrows."

The Eyeball Witch backed her up, but the Foresight Mage ignored her and continued.

"If you really want to borrow them, I need collateral. Collateral to lend only you the secret of a major undertaking being led by the Bloodsucking Mage's prized protégé—one that will someday revolutionize the world."

"Hmph. Fine. Then... how about food and water for 1,000 people for one week?"

"That's not even worth discussing. I want the magic wand Cyanos in exchange."

"What?"

Her voice came out low before she could stop it. Then she realized what he was after.

The Foresight Mage had wanted Cyanos from the beginning. He had only jumped at the chance when the Blue Witch asked for magic language materials.

The Blue Witch clicked her tongue.

"Didn't your precious future sight show you a future where I'd refuse? I told you over and over at the emergency meeting. I won't give Cyanos to anyone. I won't lend it out either."

"I heard you. But listen, Blue Witch. This is something I've said over and over at Council meetings you weren't at."

The tired middle-aged man's voice gained strength.

"At this rate, an unprecedented great famine will happen from food shortages two years from now. Even next year, nobody will starve to death, but tons of people will collapse from malnutrition."

"That's why you're pushing agriculture, forestry, and fisheries reforms."

"You still don't understand. Even if everything goes according to plan, it won't be nearly enough.

"Listen. You understand that farm machinery stopped working, right? That alone caused production efficiency to plummet. Depending on the crop, it fell 20–75%.

"Food imports stopped. That hurts too. Japan lost 62% of its food supply.

"The chemical fertilizer supply was cut by 100%. We can't farm on the assumption of abundant fertilizer anymore.

"The roads are cut off, and freight trains and transport trucks can't move. Crops produced in the breadbasket regions can't reach the cities anymore.

"The average age of the people working in farming, logging, and fishing was 68. Monsters ran wild, medical care stopped, and heating and air conditioning stopped in summer and winter. The elderly were the first victims. The people with know-how in those fields took devastating losses."

The Foresight Mage smoothly laid out the grim state of Japan's food production, backing it up with numbers.

Even the Blue Witch, who had monopolized a huge stockpile of food by herself—or with one other person, if she counted Ori—and had never felt much urgency about the food problem, got serious.

The post-apocalyptic age after human civilization's collapse—the age of echoes of the old civilization and looting—was coming to an end.

If they did not quickly build industries suited to the new age, exhausted humanity would take another hit from hunger.

"There's more.

"All of Japan's roughly 100,000 fishing boats broke down. Fishing is wiped out. That's 2.5 million tons in annual catch lost—food that was supposed to feed 3 million people. Whatever survives—river fishing, sea fishing, aquaculture, yachts, and boats—amounts to a drop in the bucket, even all put together.

"Farmland, stables, and fish farms that are somehow still producing get eaten up by monsters. Funny, isn't it? By the time we notice a monster appearing, rush over, and hunt it, the precious food is already in its stomach. Deer, monkeys, sparrows—ordinary pest animals are cute compared to this. The reclaimed fields in Katsushika Ward got wiped out completely.

"Seed and nursery companies literally went under, and hundreds of high-yield varieties were lost. That's damage we can't undo. It probably doesn't hit home for you, but instead of wasting time protecting the National Diet Building, you should've protected even one seed and nursery company.

"About 80% of Tokyo's total population died, and the food it needed fell by about 80% too. But food production fell by more than 80%. Listen—"

"Enough. I get it."

Fed up, the Blue Witch cut off the depressing explanation.

She herself had decided to shut herself away alone in Ome and ignore the problems outside. But hearing the grim situation laid out again left her feeling miserable.

She had taken down the giant kaiju alone and felt as though she had saved Tokyo. But in the face of the food-shortage problem coming in two years, perhaps Tokyo's destruction was only a matter of sooner or later.

"That's why I need your wand."

The Foresight Mage's voice faded again, as if talking had worn him out.

She had always thought he sounded worn out, but considering the scale of the problem he was working on, it made sense.

"We need power. Firepower, manpower, some unknown new energy source—anything. We need some convenient super-power that can solve everything all at once right now.

"We only have two years.

"I paid a ridiculous price to learn fertility magic from the Flower Witch. If you just lend me Cyanos, I can spread amplified fertility magic around, massively raise food production, and change that shitty future where over 3 million people starve to death around Tokyo alone."

"..."

The Blue Witch hesitated.

If Ome's residents were still alive, and someone had begged her to do it for Ome's 130,000 people, she would have hesitated, but eventually she would have lent Cyanos.

But being told to do it for 3 million complete strangers was too abstract to feel real to her.

The Blue Witch was the kind of woman who could stay up nursing her sick little sister for three days and nights, but who would not put even one yen into a convenience-store donation box.

Perhaps panicking at the Blue Witch's silence, the Foresight Mage pressed on desperately.

"I understand very well that you're worried Cyanos will be misused. I understand why you're hesitating, but I need it no matter what. At this point, I don't care where Cyanos came from. I absolutely won't misuse it. I'll definitely return it. So please! Lend me Cyanos!"

"The Edogawa Witch lent a magic stone to the Iruma Mage after he said something similar, and he killed her the moment she gave him the magic stone."

"Ah..."

Listening through the Eyeball Witch's familiar, the Blue Witch pictured the middle-aged man holding his head and groaning.

"Unfortunately, I can't argue back when you say that. That brat left the worst possible precedent before he died... No! Even so, please! All I can say is trust me, but I absolutely won't misuse it. I swear. Right, if you want to study magic language, I'll send the researcher themselves over instead of the materials. You could even take them hostage—no, that wouldn't be good, would it? That's going too far."

The Foresight Mage had nearly crossed a line, but barely came to his senses and stopped himself.

The fact that he decided he could not take one person hostage to save 3 million lives showed why he and the Bloodsucking Mage had been close.

It was a foolish decision if she thought only in numbers, but the Blue Witch liked him a little more.

After thinking it over, she offered a compromise.

"I'll look for a way to solve the food problem too. I can't lend you Cyanos, but will you settle for that?"

"You'll think about it? Come on."

"I have an idea. I didn't pull Cyanos out of nothing."

The Blue Witch's words, meant to buy trust while hinting at a trump card and stopping just short of revealing Ori's existence, left the Foresight Mage agonizing for quite a while.

But true to his name, the Foresight Mage could see the future. To look ahead at the result of his choice, he chanted an incantation.

"Tell me[^1], will there be a full moon next month?"

The Foresight Mage bragged that he could see the future, but nobody except him knew what he could actually see, or how much.

Nobody knew what he had seen with that magic just now.

The Blue Witch had no intention of breaking her promise. The Foresight Mage should not have seen any future where the deal went badly for him.

Even so, the future was uncertain. The Blue Witch asked the man who had seen the future for the result.

"What did you see?"

"It kinda looks okay. Then it's a promise."

She stared in disbelief at his slurred, child-regressed voice.

She wondered if someone else was speaking, but none of the other witches, who should have been listening nearby, reacted. It seemed to be his voice, changed beyond recognition.

"Hey, what happened? Are you okay?"

"Eahh... boku's fine!"

"You definitely had magic backlash..."

Then the Flame Witch gently soothed the middle-aged man: "Okay, off to the break room. Be a good boy." The Foresight Mage's voice faded away.

While the Council witches who had silently listened to the exchange whispered among themselves, the Eyeball Witch spoke, openly moved.

"Hey, Ao-chan. Won't you come to the meetings every month after all? It's been a long time since I saw a negotiation settle this peacefully. You both said what you thought, heard each other out, and met each other halfway. Onee-san's getting all choked up."

"I'm not coming again... probably. Later."

Before keeping the conversation going got her dragged into a messy political drama, the Blue Witch cut it off and tossed the eyeball familiar relaying the meeting out the window.

The food problem is serious. But if she asks Ori, he'll surely come up with something.

After all, he's the man who made the magic wand Cyanos, which holds the power to transform the world.
