Solving the food crisis had long been the Foresight Mage's dearest wish.

The Foresight Mage had originally been an ordinary office worker.

He lived alone and was unmarried, cut back on luxuries, and steadily saved money. He dreamed of retiring early in his forties, buying a small house in the country, and spending his retirement farming on sunny days and reading on rainy ones.

But in his late thirties, just when years of patience had finally put that dream within reach, the Gremlin Disaster struck.

The savings in his account, his digital investments, his stocks—it all vanished. By the time he finally woke from the coma that came with becoming a mage and went to work, his company was deserted.

With his life plan in ruins, the Foresight Mage was like an empty shell for a while.

But as he saved a neighbor in company housing from monsters, ended up helping people in shelters attacked by monsters, received sweets from a child he'd saved, and, with refugees whose homes had burned down, fed scrap wood into a drum can in a park, lit it, and kept warm, a sense of duty slowly grew in him.

He can see the future. His body is tough and strong, and he can answer the voices of people asking for help.

In this ruined world, if he doesn't do it, who will?

The Foresight Mage began helping people of his own free will, not because circumstances swept him along.

As he became famous as a mage, his efforts to help others grew in scale.

Sometimes people mocked him, saying things like, "That's just a childish hero complex," "It's hypocrisy," or "That old guy is trying so hard because he wants people to fawn over him." But the sharpest tongues always belonged to people who never helped anyone and put themselves first. They were idiots who sneered at even putting on a show of doing good, let alone actually doing any.

Nasty criticism hurt him as much as it hurt anyone else, but he also had ordinary joys.

The Foresight Mage had not been selflessly serving society for the sake of peace.

Being treated like a hero felt good. Doing good felt satisfying. When people fawned over him, he felt proud.

The Foresight Mage could see the future, but he worked himself ragged for immediate praise. For starters, he focused on the obvious job of keeping the peace in his home district of Bunkyo Ward.

The one who gave the Foresight Mage a long-term view was the Bloodsucking Mage, who coordinated the Tokyo Witches' Council.

The Bloodsucking Mage talked him into using a powerful foresight spell he'd always avoided for fear of feedback damage from magic backlash. It let him see three orbital periods ahead—three Earth years when cast on Earth.

The Foresight Mage had vaguely imagined "annihilation by monsters" and "a large-scale war between Transcendents" as the worst possibilities. Instead, what he saw filled him with urgency and drove him to do everything he could to avert an "unprecedented great famine caused by food shortages."

The Foresight Mage worked even more desperately than he had as an office worker.

He had warned the Tokyo Witches' Council about the food crisis again and again, but the only person who truly understood the threat was the Foresight Mage himself. He alone had vividly "seen" the living hell where people ate people to stave off hunger.

The Foresight Mage launched one policy after another to solve the food crisis. With the Bloodsucking Mage working behind the scenes, he managed to put some of them into practice.

After repeated meetings with the Mermaid Witch, whose intelligence had declined and who had difficulty holding a conversation because she had fused with an electric eel, he somehow managed to communicate with her and partially revive Tokyo Bay's fishing industry.

He reclaimed Katsushika Ward, which had been leveled right after the Gremlin Disaster and had remained deserted for a long time, and turned it into a large farm. It was later wiped out by monsters eating the crops.

He encouraged widespread use of human-waste compost and container gardens, pushing household-level increases in food production.

He ran all over the place to secure seeds and seedlings, protect agricultural workers, and pour enormous resources into restoring farming tools that did not use electricity or machinery, along with forgotten know-how.

Even while the Foresight Mage threw himself into the food problem, he had to keep Bunkyo Ward safe just as he always had.

And the food problem was not the only thing the Foresight Mage dealt with.

One accomplishment only he could ever know about was finding a con man among the religious figures who eagerly volunteered in Bunkyo Ward—a man certain to become the founder of a murderous cult with 100,000 members in the future—and secretly killing him before his preaching and brainwashing began.

The workload would have been enough to kill an ordinary person ten times over, but the sturdy body his transformation had given him kept him alive.

The Foresight Mage had worked himself so hard under such extreme stress that his face aged ten years in a single year. Thanks to his efforts, Bunkyo Ward had become famous as one of Tokyo's top one or two safest, most livable communities. People hoping to move there from other districts showed up every day.

Bunkyo Ward's streets were as well preserved as Ome, where the Blue Witch swiftly and perfectly hunted monsters. Its safety had drawn independent technicians—carpenters, water and sewer workers, blacksmiths, auto mechanics, and others—and repairs and renovations were underway, though nowhere near enough to meet demand.

Wrecked cars that had filled the roads were being removed and dismantled, and old technology was being restored. Charcoal-powered vehicle transport using the former Tokyo Metro Marunouchi Line was scheduled to begin operations next year.

Yet even Bunkyo Ward, Tokyo's hope as it advanced step by step toward recovery, was supposed to fall to famine and be wiped out two years later.

That future changed thanks to the historic achievement of the magic-language research team he had taken in after they fled Minato Ward.

The Foresight Mage had worried a great deal about how much he could trust the Blue Witch, who had said she had a lead, and whether the last member of the magic-language research team, Ohinata Kei from Ome, might be abducted by the Blue Witch. But when he heard that the fertility-magic research was complete, none of it mattered anymore.

"You did well, Ohinata-kun. You really, really did well...!"

"Whaaa!?"

The overjoyed Foresight Mage tossed Professor Ohinata high into the air again and again in celebration, leaving her eyes darting in alarm.

Each time she nearly hit her head on the office ceiling, Professor Ohinata squeezed her eyes shut and curled up. But the Foresight Mage eventually came to his senses and put her down on the floor.

He had foreseen Professor Ohinata happily delivering a report to him in his office. But foresight only showed him images. Without sound, he hadn't known what she would report and couldn't be certain.

Professor Ohinata smoothed down her messy hair, flattened the wrinkles in the papers she had clutched in her hands, cleared her throat, and held out the report.

"Ahem. This is the report. If you recite it as written here, anyone can use the fertility-magic bypass incantation. But it will be difficult to pronounce it accurately just by reading the words. It will be safest, surest, and most efficient if I supervise pronunciation practice."

"Yeah. Let me say it as many times as I want. You really did well."

"It was only possible because of the prior research by my father and our predecessors. Well, um. I will continue researching magic language. Please do not hesitate to call on me if anything comes up."

"Yeah. I'll tell you later what reward you've earned, Ohinata-kun. I promise it'll be worthy of your achievement."

"Please don't worry about it. Excuse me."

Professor Ohinata politely bowed to the Foresight Mage and hurried out of the office. Though still so young, Ohinata Kei led the field of magic linguistics. She was busy too, though not as busy as the Foresight Mage.

She really is an exceptional girl. Her reputation as a genius is no exaggeration; most geniuses couldn't have pulled off what she's done. In a world where almost all the news is about things falling apart, she's one of the few rays of hope.

He doesn't know how every problem was solved in just one day, but he doesn't care anymore.

The future where a great famine devastates Japan has been averted.

That fact is enough.

Because he could see the future, the Foresight Mage had spent so long struggling against a dark one. Now a bright future had opened before him. He trembled, his eyes burning.

But even while he wiped his eyes with his sleeve, the next job arrived.

The Foresight Mage was without question the busiest man in Japan. He had no time to enjoy the afterglow of victory.

The clerk who came into the room just as Professor Ohinata left put a stack of documents with sticky notes on his desk, flipped through them, and rapidly rattled off an explanation.

"Because of the new fuel restrictions, some ward residents are not boiling their drinking water. The medical team reports an increase in patients complaining of stomach pain and diarrhea. I have summarized here a plan for the administration to boil water in bulk and distribute it, and a policy proposal to increase fuel allocations. Shinagawa Ward seems to have fuel to spare, so I would like you to tell Flame Witch-sama to send us fuel at the next Tokyo Witches' Council meeting or so. That should get us through for the time being. And then—"

"Hold on, Matsuo-kun."

The Foresight Mage cut off the Food Team administrator's nonstop explanation and pushed the documents back.

Matsuo, who had been burned out of Minato Ward during the giant kaiju incident and taken an administrative job in Bunkyo Ward, was a capable man. But he tended to disregard rules and convention.

"This kind of thing is a problem. Send documents through my secretary. Bringing them to me piecemeal holds up the work."

The Foresight Mage held all the political decision-making power in Bunkyo Ward.

That was because he could use foresight magic to see the results of decisions in advance and make the best choice.

Still, if the Foresight Mage handled even low-priority decisions like searching for lost pets, he could have had any number of bodies and it still wouldn't have been enough. His secretary sifted through the information, sorted it by importance, and summarized it. Thanks to that, the workload was somehow kept down to merely enough to kill an ordinary person from overwork several times.

Bringing documents without going through the secretary went against the rules. It might be an annoying procedure for Matsuo, but it was necessary for the Foresight Mage, who was always on the verge of drowning in a sea of work.

When the Foresight Mage cut off his request, Matsuo openly frowned.

"Bloodsucking-sama would've made an exception."

"..."

Seeing the Foresight Mage at a loss for words, Matsuo suddenly realized what he had said and scratched his head awkwardly.

"Excuse me. I went too far. I understand. I'll go through the secretary."

The Foresight Mage watched Matsuo's awkward retreat and squeezed the fountain pen a great mage had given him long ago—so long ago now that it felt like the distant past—hard enough to crack it.

Again.

Even though months had passed since his death, many people still longed for the Bloodsucking Mage.

Even the Foresight Mage, who kept working himself to the bone, was second-rate compared to the Bloodsucking Mage. The looks of disappointment and dismay that seemed to ask, "You can see the future, and this is all you can do?" hurt.

The Bloodsucking Mage had successfully held together the Tokyo Witches' Council and all its difficult personalities. He persuaded them one by one and built a system for cooperating to recover from an unprecedented disaster.

Personal magnetism, political skill, combat strength, a broad view, deep knowledge. If anyone deserved to be called a Transcendent, it was a man like the Bloodsucking Mage. Even the cunning and brutal Iruma Mage had been more wary of and respectful toward the Bloodsucking Mage than anyone else.

The moment the Bloodsucking Mage died, the Tokyo Witches' Council was badly shaken. The witches had lost their anchor, and their cooperative system began to creak.

The Bloodsucking Mage had done it all as a matter of course, without magic. The Foresight Mage couldn't manage it even with magic at his disposal.

His intelligence and ability weren't enough to use foresight magic to its fullest. An ordinary man had gained magic too strong for him and was being tossed around by it. The Bloodsucking Mage was fundamentally a different caliber of person.

"I'm trying too. I'm trying too...!"

The rage he'd held down for so long slipped out through gritted teeth.

He is putting everything he has into this.

He is doing work that ordinary people could never manage.

And this is his reward: "Bloodsucking-sama would've handled it better"?

Isn't that too much?

He can even use magic to impose tyrannical rule.

He can even destroy everything, take everything, and wreck it all.

Is this what he gets for working so hard for a reward no better than an ordinary clerk's, nothing close to matching the heavy responsibility and crushing workload?

The Foresight Mage had originally been just an office worker. And yet, how many irritating people act as if it's only natural for him to be a first-rate politician? Then you try doing the same thing!

"No, calm down. I'm doing well. I'm fine."

Realizing his mind was tilting in a bad direction, the Foresight Mage took a deep breath.

There are people who understand him.

The Eyeball Witch has taken the lead in organizing Tokyo Witches' Council meetings and looks out for the other witches in all sorts of ways. After the Bloodsucking Mage's death, she's a friend who shares his hardships.

His secretary is young, but learns the job like a dry sponge soaking up water and supports him devotedly. Not once has he seen disappointment or dismay from the secretary.

He isn't asking for luxuries like better food or more rest.

He only wants to be praised more, recognized more, and fawned over more. Is that really such an unreasonable wish?

The Foresight Mage had been riding high after the completion of the fertility-magic research, only for Matsuo's words to kill his mood and get badly under his skin. But he sank those bottled-up feelings deep inside himself again, gave a long sigh, and returned to work.

The fertility-magic bypass incantation would break through the biggest problem before him: the food shortage. Spreading it would make his workload explode temporarily, but once they got past the peak, one burden would be off his shoulders.

There were various ways to spread the fertility-magic bypass incantation rapidly throughout Japan, but every option required the cooperation of Professor Ohinata, who had developed it.

The Foresight Mage made several draft plans and sent them to Professor Ohinata. While he was at it, he asked whether there was anything she wanted.

He had already made all kinds of special arrangements for Professor Ohinata because she was in charge of such an important project: high-quality food rations, priority access to medical care, a private bath for her exclusive use, guards assigned to her laboratory and home, and more. But an achievement this big called for a special bonus.

Professor Ohinata had handled a responsibility far too heavy for a twelve-year-old girl, and she'd done an incredible job.

No request was too selfish. The Foresight Mage would do everything he could to make it happen.

It was the only way the adults who'd had no choice but to entrust Japan's future to a child could repay her.

Professor Ohinata politely declined the bonus with manners far beyond her years and said something genuinely admirable: "I want you to use my reward for Bunkyo Ward." But that only made the Foresight Mage more determined.

At an age when she should have been making selfish demands, giving her parents trouble, and entering a rebellious phase, she was showing devotion rarely found even in adults.

It made him grateful, and sad.

It made the Foresight Mage unbearably sad that children couldn't freely ask for what they wanted, that society had grown harsh enough to force them not to.

The Foresight Mage kept pestering her about what she wanted. In the end, he half threatened her into agreeing: "If you don't accept a special reward, it'll create a bad precedent that people who work hard don't get rewarded." Ohinata finally said timidly:

"Then... I want to become a university professor. Like my father."

Professor Ohinata smiled shyly and told him of that sweet, childlike dream. Her simple innocence moved the Foresight Mage, and he threw himself into making it come true.

Fortunately, Bunkyo Ward was dotted with universities and other educational institutions, all in good condition.

Brandishing "restarting school education" as a pretext, the Foresight Mage sent people to renovate a university that was especially well preserved and named it Tokyo Magic University. He appointed Ohinata Kei as its inaugural president and first chief professor.

At first, restarting school education had been nothing more than a pretext for granting Professor Ohinata's wish. But as residents voiced their hopes and Professor Ohinata excitedly asked, "Should I make entrance exam questions or something?" he realized it had become a bigger deal than expected.

He had planned to gather children more or less at random and have them attend as students. But he changed course and, after consulting with his advisors, made it part of official policy.

In other words, Tokyo Magic University would teach the fertility-magic bypass incantation.

Foresight magic showed him that if he sent Professor Ohinata around the country to teach fertility magic, she would get caught in conflicts between witches or monster attacks along the way and lose her life.

That was extremely hard to avoid. Even if she escaped death once, she would get caught up in another incident in a different region.

Tokyo, on the other hand, was relatively safe thanks to what remained of the Bloodsucking Mage's political achievements. And the Blue Witch had declared that she would protect Professor Ohinata and was keeping everyone in line. Nothing much was likely to happen.

They would keep Professor Ohinata at the lectern of Tokyo Magic University, bring in students from all over, and have her lecture them all at once. That was the safest and most efficient way.

First, after careful planning and preparation, he had the Information Division's Letterpress Printing Section print a huge number of advertisements saying, "Magic University Opens, Students Wanted," then recruited an outstanding first cohort.

Until then, magic had almost been the exclusive domain of Transcendents and monsters.

First, incantations were hard to pronounce and included unpronounceable sounds. In other words, they were difficult—and sometimes impossible—to learn.

Second, while the amount varied from person to person, most people had little magic power. Even the shooting-magic core spell `Fire[ア゙ー]`[^1]—written to mean `Fire` but read as `ア゙ー`—used the least magic power of any spell with human-pronounceable sounds, yet a single use drained them and made them faint.

Third, many witches kept their own magic secret and didn't want their incantations spread.

People resented being shut out of magic, the new technology of the age. When a chance to learn it finally came, they rushed to be first.

Tokyo Magic University had 30 places and more than 6,000 applicants. The Foresight Mage put them through strict examinations. There was no age limit, but the tests sifted them out.

First came an intelligence test and an explanation-skills test.

The first cohort at Tokyo Magic University had to master the fertility-magic bypass incantation in a short time. Once they had learned it, they had to switch to teaching it.

If Professor Ohinata taught fertility magic alone, she wouldn't reach enough people in time to avert the famine. Each of her 30 students had to teach 30 people, producing 930 teachers in total—the initial 30 plus 900 pupils—and then all 930 had to teach 30 more each. They needed that kind of multiplicative growth.

The first cohort would be the first 30 in that chain. They needed the intelligence to master the incantation quickly, correctly, and reliably, as well as the ability to explain or teach it well.

Next came the magic-power test.

Both the original fertility magic and its bypass incantation were low-cost magic that used about as much magic power as `Fire[ア゙ー]`. But if the first cohort, expected to serve as teachers, fainted after reciting it only once, they couldn't even demonstrate it properly.

Recitations that sounded like screaming beavers echoed through the exam hall. Only candidates with enough magic power to recite it at least five times without fainting remained. Those who fainted failed and were carried out of the hall.

Last came the articulation test.

Even intelligent candidates with enough magic power would struggle with magic language's difficult, un-Japanese pronunciation if their articulation was poor.

The fertility-magic bypass incantation could be pronounced by the human vocal tract, so anyone could learn it given enough time. But the first cohort needed to learn it quickly and reliably as future teachers. Poor articulation was a major disadvantage.

Applicants took the magic language pronunciation test devised by Professor Ohinata. In the end, 30 first cohort students passed, but those who failed only because they had trouble with articulation received provisional places in the second cohort.

The recruitment, selection, and education of Tokyo Magic University's first cohort all went smoothly.

That was only natural. The Foresight Mage had removed every likely obstacle beforehand.

For example, he had seen a future where one ward resident became the source of a malicious, baseless rumor that "ordinary people who use magic turn into monsters," a rumor that would gain a huge number of believers. He fabricated a convenient charge against that resident and had them jailed in advance.

The Foresight Mage wasn't all-powerful enough to foresee every danger ahead, and seeing the future often showed him situations he couldn't do anything about. This time, though, he could.

Everything was proceeding smoothly. His work to keep it that way was paying off.

Once the first cohort finished training and was sent to survivor communities across the country as teachers, fertility-magic users would start increasing on their own everywhere.

The food problem was serious in every region. People who could use fertility magic would definitely be valued in every community.

The development of a single new spell could remake the world.

The possibilities of magic research were bottomless. Until then, the Foresight Mage had only invested in magic linguistics and hadn't had the resources for other research. But once the food problem was solved, he could catch his breath and have a little room to spare.

The brighter mood spread. Color returned not just to the Foresight Mage's face, but to the faces of the Bunkyo Ward Office staff. They had room to breathe. When the Foresight Mage came out of the nap room after midnight and found the ward office completely dark, every light off, it even moved him. No one stared at the morning sun with a dead face anymore, counting off dates on their fingers.

For the first time in a long while, the Foresight Mage woke refreshed and nightmare-free. He stretched wide in his office chair. The grandfather clock showed that three whole hours had passed.

His secretary, who was sorting documents at another desk with black-stained fingers, noticed the Foresight Mage had woken and politely bowed.

"Good morning, Foresight-sama."

"Did I sleep too long?"

"No. If you're tired, would you like to go home for a while?"

"We can't spare that kind of time."

Being able to nod off at all is good enough. Wanting to lie down in a soft bed is asking too much.

While he and his secretary discussed that day's schedule in his office, Matsuo walked in looking cheerful. The Foresight Mage flinched, and when his secretary saw it, her smile froze.

Matsuo was a capable man. But he tended to look down on the Foresight Mage, who found him hard to deal with.

"Foresight-sama, there were flaws in the levee-repair plan, so I prepared a revised proposal. Please look it over."

"There's no need to revise it. You probably thought the order was strange, but that's the best option. I saw the future, so I'm sure."

"Really? Do we need to use this many people for something like this?"

"I can explain, but it'll take a long time. You'll understand once we carry it out."

"...You are not using foresight magic as cover to force through a mistake in the plan, are you?"

"..."

Matsuo's words stung. The Foresight Mage clutched his chest and fell silent. His secretary, who had listened without saying anything, suddenly stood, walked briskly over, tapped Matsuo on the shoulder, and politely motioned him toward the door.

"Matsuo-san, come this way for a moment."

"Please leave it for later. Right now, I—"

"This. Way."

The secretary said it a second time in a gentle, calm voice that left no room to argue. Matsuo reluctantly followed her out of the office.

The moment the office door shut, two loud, slap-like sounds came from outside, as if an enraged secretary had struck an adult man's cheeks with full force.

"!?"

The Foresight Mage flinched, his eyes darting in shock, as he heard the secretary, who always wore a gentle, graceful smile in front of him, shout furiously at Matsuo. Her voice was so loud there had been no point in stepping outside. It came straight through the door, every word reaching him.

The secretary's furious lecture ended in less than a minute.

The door opened again, and the two returned.

Matsuo came back half in tears, both cheeks bright red and badly swollen, with a handprint on each.

"F-Foresight-sama. I am sorry. I said something rude without knowing how hard you work..."

"Matsuo-san? Is that how you apologize? Your head is too high. Bow lower."

"Eek."

Seeing the secretary smile and lightly wave her hand, Matsuo was completely terrified. He got down on all fours, pressed his forehead against the floor, and fully prostrated himself.

"I am terribly sorry. I did not know my place. I will never be disrespectful again."

"Ah, yeah. I know Matsuo-kun has been doing his best in his own way. It's fine."

"That's what he says. Good for you, isn't it?"

The secretary, wearing a smile full of compassion, gently patted Matsuo's back. Matsuo gingerly got up, shrank into himself, and left the office.

The Foresight Mage didn't need foresight magic to know what would happen. Matsuo would never again say anything that gouged out the Foresight Mage's heart.

"Should we suspend him? That one has done this more than once or twice, hasn't he?"

"No need. He did it because he was thinking about the future in his own way. If he'd said it out of malice, I would've fired him long ago."

The secretary opened her mouth, but ultimately said nothing. She bowed and returned to her desk.

After they quietly worked through documents together for a while, the Foresight Mage finally put the thought that had been stuck in his throat into words.

"Also."

"Yes?"

"If Bloodsucking were alive, he'd have handled it better. It's true that I'm not capable enough."

"That is not true."

The secretary immediately rejected his moment of weakness.

The secretary looked the Foresight Mage straight in the eye and said with fierce conviction:

"Foresight-sama was the one who saved me. It was not Bloodsucking Mage-sama."

"But—"

"Noise like that is not even worth listening to. Foresight-sama is the greatest mage in the world. I have watched you from closer than anyone, so I guarantee it."

"..."

The secretary had idolized the Foresight Mage ever since he saved her from monsters one year ago. She was always on his side. That was exactly why he should not take her words at face value.

He knew that in his head, but the secretary's direct words went straight to his heart.

"Thank you. For everything."

"Not at all. It is an honor to be of use to you, Foresight-sama."

Before he knew it, the dark mass that had built up deep inside him had melted away completely.

All it took was encouragement from his secretary to blow away his pent-up complaints and worries at once.

Even he had to laugh at how easy he was to please.

Even though they've averted a hell of starvation, the world is still nowhere near peaceful.

Even so, the world is steadily getting better. Not by chance, but through people's constant efforts.

The world still needs the Foresight Mage. Being relied on and praised feels good.

But this crushing workload can't go on forever.

He'll spread magic. He'll help people build their own strength. Then, once they can maintain a decent level of peace without relying on the personal military power of Transcendents who act on their whims, he'll finally retire early for good and live a slow life farming in the country...

The Foresight Mage smiled at the future he pictured and looked forward to what Tokyo Magic University would become.
