The Blue Witch woke up in her quiet house again today, washed her face, combed her hair, and cleaned the grave in the backyard. It was her usual routine, unchanged every morning.

Even before she was the Blue Witch, when people called her Aoyama Hiyori, she had liked peaceful mornings.

She wanted her little sister to wake her gently. It would have been nice to hug her when she crawled under the covers and go back to sleep.

Then she would get up at her own pace and slowly start the day with her family, enjoying toast with plenty of butter, sausages, and coffee with milk.

But that would never happen again.

After finishing a tasteless canned breakfast, the Blue Witch checked that the handgun she had stripped from a police officer's corpse worked. It was a backup weapon in case her magic power ever ran out. She slipped it into the holster on her thigh, out of sight. She made sure there were bandages in her pouch, then went to get the magic stone she used on patrol before heading outside. She was taken aback to find a magic wand, not a magic stone, in the place where she always kept it.

The magic wand Cyanos had been delivered a few days ago, but she still wasn't used to seeing it.

Ori Kenshi, a Wand Maker who was terrified of people and boasted freakish dexterity, was the first trustworthy person she had met in a long time.

His personality had its problems, but he had no malice. He had no sense of danger, either. It was impressive that he'd survived this long like that.

The magic wand sales agreement with Okutama's strange creature suited the Blue Witch just fine too.

After all, it was practically the same as getting exclusive distribution rights for the strongest weapon by a mile.

From now on, small clashes with other witches and mages will be easy to settle. If she wants, she can even use magic wands as bait to move people and control the balance of power in different places.

The Blue Witch was based in Ome, in northwestern Tokyo.

Having failed to save any of the citizens she was supposed to protect, the Blue Witch hunted the monsters that damaged the city. At least she could protect the hollow remnants of her memories.

What is the point of protecting houses with no one left to return to them?

There's nothing beyond that.

Even so, the Blue Witch stayed in Ome like a mother wolf who kept watch forever over a nest that had lost its pups. She could not help it.

With a magic wand, Ome's defenses will be stronger.

She owed Ori. In days when everything broke down, rotted, and disappeared, one broken memory from the peaceful days had come back to her. How happy that had made her!

As her benefactor had asked, the Blue Witch had started vetting customers to sell magic wands to.

She will sell to friendly factions and not sell to hostile ones. That alone will let her shape the balance of power to suit herself.

The Blue Witch intended to make full use of the broker role that had fallen into her lap. She was not good at politics, but if the payoff was big enough, it was worth considering.

If magic wands make a name for themselves as important weapons that change the balance of power, Ori will be satisfied. If the balance of power changes and Ome's defenses become stronger, the Blue Witch will be satisfied too.

Now that's Win-Win.

Gripping the magic wand Cyanos, the Blue Witch went out to patrol the city again today.

She hunted monsters that had entered from other areas and monsters that had appeared naturally in her own area.

Sometimes she hunted thieves who ignored the warning signs and tried to steal Ome's supplies, or people with the nerve to settle there without permission. Usually, she dumped them outside the area half-dead. But if they were brazen and showed no sign of remorse, she showed no mercy.

She'd had three fights against small fry that morning.

None of them was a good enough target to test the magic-amplification effect of the magic wand Cyanos.

In her first fight with a monster after it had been delivered, the Blue Witch had used her lowest-power ice-spear magic on a carnivorous plant that had grown as large as a compact car. She had lost control of the abnormally amplified magic, piercing through five houses at once and bringing them down. It was overkill.

She couldn't sell a magic wand without knowing how the product performed.

Ori's specification sheet listed the catalog specs. But even if it said, "A major amplification of magic through a seven-layer core," she wouldn't know exactly how much it amplified magic until she tried it.

She wanted a good target for a test shot, but powerful monsters never appeared at times like that.

With the magic wand in hand, the Blue Witch jumped from roof to roof. After spending several hours carefully checking every corner of the city from above to make sure there were no intruders, she went home and had an early lunch. She'd looted every bit of food from the Iruma Mage's territory while she was there to kill him, so she still had plenty stockpiled.

She was thinking that once she'd eaten, she'd take more food to Okutama and throw in some fruit from the tree at Nakamura-san's house next door when something struck the window with a dull thud.

When she turned around, a floating eyeball was ramming the living-room window.

It was the Eyeball Witch's familiar.

Annoyed that it had apparently come for more pointless small talk, she opened the window for it. The eyeball came inside and began relaying the Eyeball Witch's voice, though she couldn't tell where its mouth was.

"It's an emergency."

The Blue Witch raised one eyebrow as the tense voice launched in without even a greeting.

The laid-back Eyeball Witch seemed pretty desperate.

"What?"

"A giant monster has come ashore from Tokyo Bay. Right now, coastal Tokyo is a sea of fire. I need help."

"Don't ask me. Whoever runs that area can deal with it."

The Blue Witch protected Ome, well inland within Tokyo. The coast was outside her territory, and she had no interest in it.

She coldly dismissed her, but the eyeball darted its pupil around in panic and went on.

"I said a giant monster, but basically, it's a kaiju. A kaiju. It's easily 100 m long."

"Oh? That's pretty big. Then Bloodsucking must be leading some kind of joint operation."

"The Bloodsucking Mage died in battle. The Setagaya Witch ran away. Right now, the Flame Witch and Hachioji Witch are holding it back."

The unexpected news made the Blue Witch grip her magic wand tight without thinking. Until then, she'd been indifferent because it was someone else's problem. No matter how powerful the monster was, she couldn't immediately believe Bloodsucking was dead.

"Wait. Bloodsucking died? Really?"

"Really. But in exchange, every civilian in Minato Ward, where the kaiju came ashore, evacuated safely."

"I thought he was the kind of guy who wouldn't die even if you killed him... Right. So that's how he died."

Unlike her, he had died after protecting every innocent civilian he was meant to protect. She envied him a little.

He was an unlikable man who was always absorbed in playing politics, but he was worthy of respect.

The Blue Witch observed a brief moment of silence, then nodded.

"All right. As a tribute to Bloodsucking, if the kaiju comes near Ome, I'll kill it."

"I know you don't want to leave Ome, but this is really bad. How long can the Flame Witch and Hachioji Witch hold out by themselves? The Foresight Mage looks ready to break down, and the other witches won't budge... Ao-chan, could you come over here? Please. We need every fighter we can get!"

A chance to test Cyanos against a giant monster suited the Blue Witch just fine too.

But she had no intention of leaving Ome unguarded and going on an expedition for that.

Once, out of kindness, the Blue Witch had gone to Kanagawa to kill a bizarre man-eating bird that flew at the speed of sound. While she was away, the Iruma Mage attacked Ome and kidnapped all its residents.

It was a bitter memory she'd never forget.

A quick visit to a neighboring district was one thing, but she had no intention of going so far that she couldn't return right away.

"I'm not leaving here. If it comes near Ome, pull the Flame Witch and Hachioji Witch back. I'll hunt it alone."

Thinking of the two witches who had said they had been her fans since her days as a magazine model, the Blue Witch gave that warning out of kindness.

The eyeball literally opened wide and shot back.

"What are you talking about? That's impossible! You're strong, sure, but you're about as strong as the Iruma Mage, right? I'm not even asking you to coordinate with them. Just give them covering fire—"

"I said I'll do it alone. If you want to get caught in the crossfire, go ahead and fight alongside me."

"...Oh, come on! Run if it gets dangerous!"

The kaiju's roar came through the floating eyeball. As if the roar were hurrying her, the Eyeball Witch rattled off her last words and cut the connection.

The familiar blinked a greeting to the Blue Witch, then floated back out the window.

After watching the familiar go, the Blue Witch climbed onto her roof. In the distance, a giant kaiju was advancing, mowing down buildings as it came.

"Huh? It's hu—...!"

Its size surpassed everything she'd heard and left her speechless.

The kaiju looked like a black Tyrannosaurus made even more upright. The monster was so huge that she could only think her sense of perspective had gone wrong.

A body that big could destroy Tokyo just by taking a walk. Yet the kaiju gripped a giant structure that looked like a radio tower in its hand and smashed everything around it to pieces like a child throwing a tantrum.

As she watched in shock, a pillar of fire rose from the ground. The crimson flames were probably the Flame Witch's.

It was powerful magic on a scale that could burn an entire skyscraper to a crisp. But it only licked at the knee of that absurdly huge body, and the kaiju kept walking as though it didn't care about the fire at its feet at all.

This time, several chains stretched out from the ground and wrapped around the kaiju. They looked thin and unreliable from this distance, but they were the Hachioji Witch's sealing chains, known for their outstanding toughness and binding power.

But the kaiju shuddered in annoyance, swung its tail around, and fired a beam from the tip of its tail that easily melted the chains.

She started to feel dizzy.

She had seen many monsters beyond human understanding before.

She'd hunted them too, after battles to the death.

Not long after the Gremlin Disaster began, pessimists who preached the end of the world had kept insisting humanity would die out. But she had believed that wouldn't happen as long as she and the other witches were there, as long as Transcendents with powers beyond human understanding existed.

Sacrifices couldn't be avoided, but humanity would never die out. She'd believed that.

But when she saw that embodiment of violence advancing with the ground shaking beneath it, like it was crossing an empty field, her certainty wavered. A body that huge. The intelligence to swing a weapon. Even magic.

She couldn't believe what she was seeing. Would even a direct hit from a nuclear bomb fail to kill a super-creature like that?

As the Blue Witch started to lose her nerve, the feel of the magic wand in her hand brought her back to her senses.

Right.

She cannot run.

Ome lies in the monster's path.

No matter what kind of monster it is, the Blue Witch will protect the streets that still hold memories of peaceful days. If she cannot even do that, she will break.

There is hope.

Her old self would never have stood a chance, but now she has the magic wand Cyanos.

If she pours every bit of her magic power into her strongest magic and amplifies it with Cyanos, she will surely hurt it. She can at least hope that will make it change course.

Monsters were originally ordinary animals. She doesn't know what animal it once was, but it cannot be an exception.

They didn't live to destroy things.

Like wild animals, monsters ran away if they were hurt or felt threatened.

She probably cannot defeat it.

But if she can hurt it and drive it away...

As long as she defends Ome at all costs, she doesn't care if the kaiju changes course and goes wild somewhere else.

The Blue Witch took a deep breath and readied Cyanos on the roof.

The kaiju looked far away, but its strides were so big that it closed the distance faster than she'd expected.

Once the kaiju entered the range of her magic, the pillar of fire and the chains wrapped around it vanished. It looked like they had started withdrawing as she'd instructed.

At full power, her magic is hard to control.

With a magic wand's amplification on top of that, control will become almost impossible.

Even so, all she has to do is fire straight ahead. With a target that huge, she cannot miss.

The Blue Witch gathered the magic power surging through her body and began pouring it into the wand.

Magic power leaked from the Blue Witch's body, turning into cold air around her.

Frost formed at her feet, and diamond dust created by the sudden cooling of the air and changes in air pressure danced in the swirling wind.

"Ugh... ahh...!"

The Blue Witch groaned as her magic power tried to surge backward.

She hadn't even poured in half her magic power, but she'd already lost control of the magic.

The signs of the magic alone froze her house before it even activated. Icicles sprouted everywhere and grew thicker and longer.

Heavy, cold air sank into her lungs and stabbed the Blue Witch's body from the inside, though she was supposed to be resistant to cold.

There is no way she can limit the area of damage.

All she can do is point the wand forward and fire it in the kaiju's direction.

But that is enough.

With the charge complete, the Blue Witch gripped the wand's handle with fingers coated in frost and beginning to freeze. She breathed out a white cloud and smiled.

If this doesn't work, nobody can defeat that monster.

So with all her strength, every last bit of it.

Shall we put it to the test?

The skill of the world's one and only Wand Maker!

Then the Blue Witch spoke the incantation and unleashed her strongest magic, the spell that had once destroyed the Iruma Mage in a single blow.

"You, sink beneath the glacier. Sleep in the permafrost!"[^1]

A pale blue wave burst from Cyanos's magic stone with a crack like a gunshot.

The wave was invisible, but the air in its path froze and liquefied in an instant, then went beyond even that and turned into a white solid. It looked like a merciless white beam attacking its prey.

But the kaiju was no pushover.

With reflexes far too fast for its huge body, it tried to block the magic by using the radio tower in its hand as a shield.

But the white wave pierced straight through the radio tower and the kaiju. A moment later, the kaiju froze solid from head to toe.

That was not all.

The out-of-control magic exploded against the kaiju and spread through the surrounding area, sealing even the city at its feet beneath thick ice.

To top it all off, the magic reached the sky and swallowed the clouds, and unseasonal snow began to fall.

Quiet white snow fell over Tokyo, trampled by the kaiju and on the verge of being swallowed by fire and destruction.

A tranquil silence mercifully enveloped the world. The Blue Witch let out a dry laugh at the sight her own magic had created.

Who asked for this much?

Now she was the monster that would destroy the world.

"This really is overtechnology, Ori."

The Blue Witch murmured weakly, then passed out when her magic power ran dry.
