It was the height of summer, and the scorching days kept coming. The rice in my paddies had started putting out ears.

From here, grains would fill out inside those ears and swell, leading to autumn's bounty. It was an important time. Rice farming was important all the way from spring to autumn, but once the ears began to form, you had to be especially careful.

Last year, I had been too stingy with the topdressing that helped the grains fill out, so there had been a lot of light grains. This year, I spread more topdressing than I had last year.

Now that no new chemical fertilizer was being produced, highly effective fertilizer was valuable. Before the Gremlin Disaster, I could spread extra fertilizer because it would reliably increase the yield. Now I had to judge the amount and use it sparingly.

I had also tried producing organic fertilizer by fermenting human waste and food scraps into compost, but it was not going very well. Apparently, the amount of waste from living alone simply was not enough.

A compost heap made from one person's household waste was small.

If a compost heap was small, the heat produced during the composting process escaped right away, and the temperature did not rise.

If the temperature did not rise, the bacteria and parasites in the compost did not die.

If the bacteria and parasites did not die, it did not become good compost.

If I forcibly raised the temperature with fire or boiling water, I would also kill microorganisms that needed to stay alive.

That was why producing good compost while living alone was difficult.

Lately, I had added fire salamanders, so my household was now one person and three salamanders, but those guys lived on charcoal and did not poop. They only let out burps like exhaust fumes.

Apparently, fertilizer production was going well in city centers because they produced huge amounts of household waste. I could just have fertilizer brought in, but I did not want to depend too much on outside sources for the basics of my life. It would be a problem if they said, “If you want fertilizer, do this! Do that!” I know that's probably paranoia, though.

One day, while I was tending the rice paddies and working on a contraption that used insulation, vapor pressure, and a brazier to keep the temperature in the compost constant semi-automatically, a letter arrived from Professor Ohinata.

It started with the usual seasonal greeting, then covered what was happening in the city and answers to my questions. But I was surprised to read, “I would like to come visit you soon.”

Professor Ohinata understood well that I was bad with people, and she was very considerate about that kind of thing. I wondered what was up with her saying something like that, but then the letter went on to say, “I succeeded in stabilizing transformation magic.”

What!?

That means you can freely switch between stoat mode and beastkin mode!?

Whoa, that's insanely hype!

If she would come visit as a small, fluffy, cute stoat instead of in human form, I had no reason to refuse. I eagerly wrote back a letter giving her an enthusiastic welcome.

The Blue Witch, who delivered the letters, had seen the professor's transformation ahead of me too. She had given it her stamp of approval by saying, “Kei-chan as a stoat is cute too,” which made me look forward to the visit even more.

Professor Ohinata was the president of Tokyo Magic University, a professor in the Department of Magic Linguistics, and on top of that a busy adviser to the Tokyo Witches' Council. But she managed to work out her schedule and visited Okutama for the first time in a while at the beginning of September.

Seeing the white stoat come trotting out from beyond the Lost Mist at the Blue Witch's feet made me smile without thinking. Welcome, welcome to Okutama Park!

“Professor, long time no see.”

“It has been a long time, Ori-san. I'm happy to see you again!”

I crouched in front of the stoat that ran up to my feet and shook her tiny front paw.

An amulet locket pendant with its chain shortened swayed against the glossy fur on her chest.

I see. I had thought she had suddenly gone back to being a stoat, but apparently she had been researching it for a while.

Professor Ohinata's locket pendant was the amulet I had given her half a year ago.

Along with asking for a locket pendant so she could keep a photo of her father inside, she had also asked for an adjustable chain.

I had made it as requested without thinking much about why, but there had been a good reason. Apparently, she wanted an adjustable chain so she could keep wearing the necklace even when her body size changed between human and stoat form.

“I'm happy you turned back into a stoat, but are you okay with that, Professor?”

“What do you mean, am I okay with it?”

“You like being in human form, right? Oh, or do you get some special ability when you go into stoat mode?”

For example, the Dragon Witch was a transformation-magic user whose default form was human, but she was always transformed into a dragon, so that became her title.

Dragons could breathe fire without an incantation, fly, and protect themselves with tough scales. They had all kinds of powerful, useful abilities. Also, apparently the Dragon Witch's personality matched a dragon's nature, so the form felt right to her.

I wondered whether stoat mode had some advantage too, but Professor Ohinata shook her head.

“No, it does not have any special abilities. As a human, I'm sturdier, more dexterous, and faster. In this form, I get hungry more easily, there are fewer things I can eat, and I shed more too.”

“So there are no upsides at all...”

“There are benefits, you know?”

“Like what?”

“I can see Ori-san like this.”

Professor Ohinata said it with a smile, without a trace of embarrassment.

Huh...? Is that a benefit?

“What, do you like me or something?”

“Yes! Can we be friends if I look like this?”

I had meant it as a casual jab, but she came right back with an energetic, straight counterpunch. That actually freaked me out.

Eek, extroverts are scary! We should be speaking the same language, but every word she says is bursting with light. Terrifying. I really do not understand people of the light.

“Did you work hard so you could freely transform between human and stoat just to become friends with me...?”

“Yes.”

“W-Why? I know I'm saying this about myself, but I'm not exactly the kind of person anyone would want to be friends with, am I?”

When I voiced my sincere question, the Blue Witch watching us gave a deep nod, which was rude.

I'm gonna wreck you at Jenga later.

“Hmm. The reason I wanted to be friends is because I wanted to be friends.”

“Huh?”

“Ever since we first met, I have wanted to get along with you and become friends. Is that such a strange thing?”

“That's strange.”

“Kei-chan is just that kind of girl. Ori, have you never met someone like Kei-chan before?”

Maybe she sensed that our conversation was about to go in circles, because the Blue Witch cut in.

Professor Ohinata was supposed to be smart, but she was saying such incomprehensible things that I asked the Blue Witch for her take.

“I seriously don't get it. What does that mean?”

“There is no particular reason. In short, Kei-chan is sociable. You know that, right? Even with a hopeless guy like you, Ori, she wants to be friends, and she'll work hard for that.”

“You don't become friends by working hard at it. Real friends are people you naturally get along with because you click, and before you know it, you're friends. Like me and you.”

“...Well, there are friendships like that too. But that is not what friendship means to Kei-chan.”

“?????”

She said it gently, like she was explaining something to me, but I still did not get it.

So what does that mean? It's beyond me.

I had been able to properly understand and deal with the complex-fracture relationships that started with the Flame Witch's arson sex, but I could not understand Professor Ohinata's thinking.

As I held my head, Professor Ohinata wagged her tail restlessly. The Blue Witch sighed and wrapped up the conversation.

“Just follow your heart. Forget your definition of friendship for once. How did you feel when Kei-chan said she wanted to be friends?”

“I thought extroverts were scary.”

“Oh, come on... Fine. Did you hate it?”

“I... didn't hate it, I guess?”

We had been exchanging letters for years, so I knew Professor Ohinata was a good person.

Her letters were always clear and kind, and she included sweets every time. We did not click, but she was definitely a genuinely good person.

Her asking to be friends had scared me, but it had not made me uncomfortable.

“I see. I kind of get what you're trying to say.”

“Then you already know how to answer Kei-chan.”

Prompted by her, I turned back to the stoat.

I spoke to Professor Ohinata, whose tail was standing straight up as she tensed a little.

“It didn't feel bad. We can be friends.”

“Will you be friends with me?”

“Well, yeah.”

“Thank you very much! Let's be good friends!”

Professor Ohinata hopped up and down, absolutely delighted.

Cute.

If it made this cute creature that happy, being friends was fine by me.

But there was one problem too.

I spoke to the Blue Witch, who was nodding away with a satisfied look.

“Now that Professor Ohinata and I are friends, I have to rethink my relationship with you.”

“Hey, what are you about to say now...?”

“Me and you are pretty high-level friends, right? Compared with you, Professor Ohinata just became my friend, so our, uh, intimacy? Our levels are different. Isn't it weird to lump both of you together under the same word, ‘friends’?”

“Oh, is that all? Ori, you're overthinking it. We can both be friends, right? There are all kinds of friends.”

“No, I'm not satisfied with that. So from today on, Blue Witch, you're my best friend.”

The Blue Witch froze at my words.

She freezes up a lot. Because of the mask, sometimes I have no idea what she's thinking.

“...Best friend. I don't mind. But now that we've gone from friends to best friends, you should keep in mind that we might someday have a different kind of relationship too.”

“Huh? Is there something above best friends?”

“Never mind if you don't know. More importantly, if we're best friends, calling me Blue Witch is too distant. Start calling me by my family name or given name already.”

“Ah, I guess that's true. I was calling you that out of habit. Ao, Ao, Ao-what was it again? Aomori? Aoki? The thing written on the nameplate at your house?”

“It's Aoyama. Aoyama Hiyori.”

“Then Hiyori.”

“You're going with my given name that easily...? What is going on with your sense of distance? Seriously.”

I tugged the sleeve of the Blue Witch, now Hiyori, who had started muttering. I caught the still-hopping stoat with one hand, held her in my arms, and happily headed inside.

There are three of us, so let's play cards in the living room! The card games you can play with two people are pretty limited, and that was getting boring. With three people, board games are way more fun too.

I'm glad I got more friends. But I don't think I need any more than this.

After that, we spent the whole morning playing cards and board games.

Professor Ohinata was strong at both cards and board games. Her moves were strong, of course, but her table talk was strong too.

If you accepted one of her proposals that seemed beneficial to both sides and did as she said, somehow Professor Ohinata had won before you knew it. But even if you rejected Professor Ohinata's demands, for some reason you still could not win. Isn't she too strong? This is impossible.

Out of spite, I dominated at Jenga and sugoroku[^1], but Hiyori, who could only take second or third place in every game, sulked. She had actually gotten first place in one board game, but she snapped at the innocent stoat, saying, “You were going easy on me!” and declared it an invalid match herself, so it did not count.

I did not know whether Professor Ohinata had actually been going easy on her. If she had, she was way too good at it.

For lunch, I made a simple meal of salt-grilled ayu, rice cooked in a pot, lightly pickled cucumber, and miso soup. It was all homemade.

Hiyori had come over for lunch every so often, so she had no reaction. But the stoat was delighted in an exaggerated way and buried her face in the Japanese food I had served on a small plate.

Good, isn't it? Good, isn't it? I'm a good cook, and my food tastes good. When I first held a kitchen knife in home economics class in elementary school, I made an apple rabbit—realistic version—and left the teacher speechless. There are no bad cooks in the dexterity crowd.

After we finished eating and were full, we did not really feel like playing games. We naturally ended up relaxing for a little while.

While drinking after-lunch tea—Professor Ohinata had brought the tea leaves—I asked what she had been up to lately.

“Professor, I get the impression you're busy, but are you free today?”

“It's okay. I cleared my schedule for the whole day.”

“So you usually are busy, then.”

“Yes. I would say I'm quite busy.”

“What have you been doing lately? Teaching, researching?”

“Hmm. Let me see, the most interesting thing I've been working on lately is...”

The stoat crossed her tiny arms and thought.

Then Hiyori, who had been sipping tea, offered a suggestion.

“Of the things you've been working on, I thought the Nameless Epic was interesting. Why don't you tell Ori about it?”

“Oh? The Nameless Epic...!”

I had no idea what that was, but the name alone already sounded cool.

It was like my middle-school sensibilities were coming back. Back then, words like “Nameless” and “Unrivaled” made my heart race. I'm interested!

The stoat watched my expression and smiled.

“Are you interested? Then let me give you a quick overview.

The Nameless Epic Hypothesis is a hypothesis that may concern the foundation of magic linguistics. It emerged as we obtained more samples of magic incantations through the envoys from the Tohoku Hunting Association and the Hokkaido Magic Beast Farm.”

According to her, some magic incantations seemed to have chronology and connections within them.

The words of one magic's incantation linked to another's. Their magical effects were related too.

From this, they had guessed that the many magic incantations might actually be excerpts from one long passage.

“The biggest thing that led us to propose the hypothesis was death-curse magic and magic-reflection magic.

There is a type of magic called death-curse magic that curses someone to death. Its incantation goes like this.

‘I love you[ナトウ・ヤウエ]. But I'm a devil[デンニエクララバアイエン]. This is what my love looks like[フクシツワクララフイフイ・ヤウエ].’”

As she spoke, the professor wrote the translation of the incantation on a piece of notepaper.

I read the translation and recoiled. Isn't it seriously messed up that this incantation's effect is a death curse?

“Devils are bad news. Their love is way too twisted.”

“I had the same thought. Then, the incantation for the magic-reflection magic used by a witch from the Hokkaido Magic Beast Farm goes like this.

‘Love yourself before anyone else[ンシユオンウータツほにやららヤーウエ].’”

As she spoke, the professor wrote the translation of the reflection incantation beneath the translation of the death-curse incantation.

When I looked at them side by side, I could clearly see the connection—or rather, the exchange.

First,

“I love you. But I'm a devil. This is what my love looks like,”

the devil spouted that crap while trying to curse someone to death.

In response, the person under the curse said,

“Love yourself before anyone else,”

preaching at the devil while reflecting the curse, along with its twisted love, back at it.

“So here's what happened. The devil confessed, and the person being confessed to—probably some cleric-type guy—turned him down, right? It sounds like something a cleric would say.”

“Yes. That is exactly it! It works as a response in a conversation, and its magical effect reflects the death curse too.

Apparently, the witch who uses this incantation had wondered why an incantation telling someone to love themself would produce magic-reflection magic. But when you consider the incantation for death-curse magic, could it be any clearer?”

“Whoa. I see.”

This was seriously interesting.

I had thought the translations of incantations sounded poetic.

But it had never occurred to me that they really were poetry, or part of a poetic text.

Once she pointed it out, I could not see it any other way.

“At the Department of Magic Linguistics, the leading theory is that all magic incantations are quoted from one epic. We have provisionally named this grand epic with an unknown title the ‘Nameless Epic.’”

“Could that name be any better? I want to give a magic wand to whoever came up with it.”

“Haha, I'll let them know.”

The incantations only gave them tiny fragments of the Nameless Epic, so they still had no idea what the whole thing was like.

All they knew for sure was that a devil and a cleric appeared in it, and that someone became a king partway through the story.

The Nameless Epic was the source of all magic. I wanted to read it so bad.

“Tell me when they figure out the full text of the Nameless Epic. I'm super interested.”

“Yes, of course. Personally, I hope that unraveling the contents of the Nameless Epic will help us uncover the history of the Shantak Meteor Shower that caused the Gremlin Disaster. The disaster has already happened, but we cannot assume it will not happen a second or third time. It is better to know the cause.

...And actually, I'm dying to know the full text of the Nameless Epic too!”

“The incantation for freezing magic that I know is pretty sad. I wonder if there will be any salvation?”

“Ugh, right, the epic could have a bad ending. I don't like that.”

“I wonder. I do not think it will be a simple bad ending. Even if it is a bad ending, it will at least contain some kind of lesson. Of course, I would be happy if the epic turns out to have a happy ending.”

After that, we spent a while getting really excited over the mystery of the Nameless Epic.

Working with magic stones and Gremlins had a lot of depth.

But magic linguistics had just as much.

I could not wait to see where the research went from here.
