The general-purpose, mass-produced magic wands made with the Handa-style manufacturing method—the symbol of the mass-production route that was the complete opposite of mine—were gradually spreading as more production lines were added.

It had been less than half a year since standard magic-wand production began, and they were still only making around 100 a day. At this rate, it would take around eighty years to get them to the residents around Tokyo.

But every new production line added about ten wands to the daily output. The craftspeople's skills were gradually improving too, and production was getting more efficient.

Output would rise faster and faster, so it probably wouldn't be long before magic wands spread not just through Tokyo, but all over Japan.

Tokyo had built up a good reconstruction cycle.

Or rather, they'd succeeded at building a good reconstruction cycle centered on the Tokyo Witches' Council.

According to the slightly questionable information from the Dragon Witch, who flew all over the country, Tokyo Metropolis, ruled by the Tokyo Witches' Council, was the biggest of all survivor communities and had rebuilt the most.

According to a statistical survey the Eyeball Witch had done at the start of this fiscal year, Tokyo's population was now around 2.8 million. Adding up the whole Tokyo metropolitan area, it was 3.5 to 4 million. That was roughly 20% of the population at its peak.

Right after the Gremlin Disaster, there just weren't enough hands. Life had depended on huge amounts of all kinds of supplies brought in from outside Tokyo, and it collapsed when logistics stopped.

They ate through their stockpiles, worked the fields with everything they had (without machines!), desperately cleared new land, and desperate fishermen lined the docks. Even sparrows and pigeons became valuable food. They also went around collecting food from places in nearby cities that monsters had wiped out.

Even so, there wasn't enough nutrition, and Tokyo's residents gradually got thinner.

The water supply stopped, so they had to walk long distances to the river every day to fetch water. Dismantling abandoned houses for lumber to secure fuel took time and effort too. Someone always had to watch the fire (a fire in the middle of Tokyo would be no joke. There were no fire trucks!), and if a monster showed up, they dropped everything and fought desperately, risking their lives until a witch or mage arrived.

Every Tokyo resident was desperate just to survive that day. They had no spare energy to think about tomorrow, or reconstruction.

I remembered how hard it was in the early days too.

Most of each day went to gathering food and fuel, and even then it wasn't enough. Things were unstable enough to make me anxious even when I had the countryside's supplies all to myself.

In those days, things were so harsh that people who couldn't keep up died one after another. Some people, led by the Bloodsucking Mage, somehow forced out enough breathing room to invest in the future. That deserved praise.

That small, faint hope for the future bore fruit when Professor Ohinata succeeded with the fertility-magic bypass incantation.

Fertility magic more than doubled harvests from farmland, letting people who had been tied up with agricultural work do other things.

Then they could use those free hands to install simple waterworks.

Once the waterworks were in place, the people who had been spending their time hauling water could do other things too.

The number of people able to work for tomorrow instead of just surviving today exploded.

That was why they could establish a university, something unnecessary if all they cared about was surviving the day. They could build more and more magic-wand production workshops and put craftspeople and apprentices in them.

The first trigger, the fertility-magic bypass incantation, had set off a perfect chain reaction and created a positive cycle.

Well, most of what I knew about this came secondhand from the Blue Witch and Professor Ohinata. I hadn't actually been in the middle of the dramatic reconstruction (I had only pitched in here and there from the safe, peaceful countryside), so I didn't know how closely what I heard matched reality.

The giant Gremlin that had rolled into my hands that day was one result of that positive chain too.

Back when the Blue Witch defeated the giant kaiju, the huge, thick glacier that swallowed up an entire city could only be melted with magical fire.

So the Flame Witch had been steadily working on thawing it every day. But ordinary people started learning fire magic, and general-purpose, mass-produced magic wands began to spread, speeding up the thawing work. What had initially been expected to take three years was shortened, and it was finished in two and a half.

Amazing.

But what the hell was up with the Blue Witch, creating in a single shot a huge glacier that took two and a half years to thaw? Was she some bug in the world or what?

It sounded like there were still all kinds of annoying problems left after the thawing was done, like relocating the residents. Apparently there was also a big fight over the giant kaiju's corpse.

First, the Dragon Witch took the massive amount of raw kaiju meat.

Most monster meat wasn't fit for people to eat. It gave humans an upset stomach. But witches and mages could eat it normally, so the Dragon Witch, who had come to sneak bites and literally gotten a taste for it, took on disposing of all the meat. Apparently she breathed fire and smoked whatever she couldn't eat raw all at once.

I was kind of conflicted, but it worked out. If they took too long disposing of all that meat people couldn't eat, it would rot in no time and become a breeding ground for disease. She was useful once in a while.

But they fought over everything except the meat.

The kaiju's tough scales, which half-baked magic couldn't even budge, rapidly began to deteriorate as their magic power drained out after they were thawed and removed. Even so, they were useful. The sturdy bones that had supported the kaiju's giant body were valuable resources too. The two 80 mm giant Gremlins taken from its tail and chest were priceless as well.

The residents of Hamura City and their administrator, the Tobacco Witch, claimed ownership. The Blue Witch showed interest too, and the Flame Witch and Hachioji Witch, who had risked their lives holding the kaiju back, demanded shares. The Setagaya Witch, who had run away from the kaiju partway through, shamelessly joined the scramble too, and the situation became a complete mess.

In the end, the Eyeball Witch mediated, and they decided to divide everything fairly.

Of course, they fought over what fair meant too. Should it go by level of contribution? How do you judge contribution? Split it by the number of witches? Apparently, it got all the way to the point where the Blue Witch nearly readied Cyanos. Terrifying. I would never want to attend a meeting like that. Even if I had ten stomachs, they'd all get wrecked.

The Blue Witch had killed several dozen residents who had failed to escape when the fallout from her magic froze an entire city. Even so, she was the one who ultimately killed the kaiju.

The fact that, at a low estimate, tens of thousands more would have died if the Blue Witch hadn't defeated it was given serious weight, so she snatched two giant Gremlins as her share.

At Professor Ohinata's request, she promptly donated one of them to Tokyo Magic University, where it became perfect joint research material for the Department of Monster Studies and the Department of Gremlin Engineering.

And the remaining one ended up in my hands.

I put the giant Gremlin on a stand in my home workshop and looked it over carefully in admiration.

“It's huge... 80 mm, right? It's not as good as a magic stone, but it's pretty too. This thing's nice.”

“You like it? Should we put off the board game until tomorrow?”

“Hm? Oh. I mean, is it really okay for me to have this? It was your share, right?”

“I wouldn't use it if I kept it. I have Cyanos. I only secured it because I figured Ori would want it.”

“Seriously? Let's stay friends forever! Friends are the best!”

“You're way too easily bought...”

The Blue Witch was exasperated, but I meant it.

I had thought I'd spend my whole life without understanding the meaning or significance of friends, but now that I had one, it wasn't bad.

Or maybe that wasn't it? This weirdly happy feeling, more than just getting something expensive—was that not because I had a friend, but because it was the Blue Witch?

I only had one friend, so I had no clue.

The Blue Witch left after setting down a two-player board game she said she had dug out of the ruins, so I faced the giant Gremlin again and looked at it carefully.

The biggest Gremlins found so far had been 28 mm electricity-derived ones, found at power plants around the country. The monster-derived ones were in the 40 mm range.

Compared to that, this one was 80 mm. It was a big one that smashed the previous record.

Monster-derived Gremlins shared two traits: they were close to spherical even unprocessed, and they were colored. Its unique bluish-white color looked like ice, but when the light hit it differently, it also looked like blue fire at high temperatures.

Hmmmm. Beautiful.

I could polish this up and display it. It would look awesome beside Okutameteorite, the sacred object of my magic-wand workshop.

But with a Gremlin this big, I could try all kinds of processing methods I'd never been able to try before because other Gremlins were too small.

What should I do? What a luxurious problem.

I spent the entire night staring blankly at the giant Gremlin and thinking it over, but in the end I decided to use it for processing experiments.

Using it as workshop decoration was tempting too, but Tokyo Magic University was using theirs for research. I got a little scared that if I just lazily pursued my hobby, I'd fall behind technologically. Professor Handa's research team was seriously no joke.

If both giant Gremlins had been mine, I could have displayed one and used the other for experiments. Oh well. Can't be helped.

I decided to use the giant Gremlin for further research into Moebius processing, the hottest thing going at the time.

There was a lot to Moebius processing. The giant Gremlin was worth using for it, and there were experiments I couldn't try without one.

The important thing was that the behavior of an incantation changed depending on the Gremlin used to cast it.

That behavior could be classified like this.

① Using magic with a normal-shaped Gremlin

・Two unrelated people using magic with one Gremlin at the same time → only one activated. The other one failed.

・Twins using magic with one Gremlin at the same time → both activated, but abnormal vibration occurred.

② Using magic with a Moebius ring Gremlin

・Two unrelated people using magic with one Gremlin at the same time → both activated at the same time.

・Twins using magic with one Gremlin at the same time → both activated at the same time.

In short, a Moebius ring was a magically stable shape that could even handle two spells at the same time. It didn't amplify or weaken magic, but it could output magic at exactly 1.00 times the power.

But there was more to this principle.

It was what happened when twins used the same magic on a Moebius ring Gremlin at the same time.

At that point, the magic-power cost got split evenly among them.

Take the freezing-magic core incantation “Freeze[ヴアアラー],” which had actually been tested and confirmed, as an example.

When twins held a Moebius ring Gremlin together and both recited “Freeze[ヴアアラー]” at the same time, only one freezing beam came out. Both its power and magic-power cost were for one casting by one person.

But that magic-power cost was divided in two between the twins.

In other words, the two of them had cooperated to cast a single spell. Each of them could cast it for half the usual magic-power cost.

They had also experimented with triplets. In that case, one spell activated while each person only spent one-third of the magic power.

They hadn't been able to experiment with quadruplets or more because they couldn't find the people, but the experiments with twins and triplets were more than enough to guess what was happening.

In theory, with a hundred-tuplet set, you could split the magic-power cost a hundred ways.

Even powerful magic that humans could cast but that cost too much magic power to be practical for anyone but witches and mages could be used this way, with people cooperating and sharing the cost.

One pair of twins had apparently used this cooperative incantation, which Professor Ohinata called a “chorus,” to cast magic neither had enough magic power to activate alone.

Very, very interesting.

Two people matching their voices and combining their power to cast magic! Cool as hell! And useful too!

But the bottleneck was perfectly matching vocal quality.

Because of that limitation, a “chorus” was restricted to exceptions like twins, triplets, and the Hell Witch.

That was still amazing, but I wanted to see a hundred people join in one huge chorus and cast some massive magic.

So I planned to use the giant Gremlin to get rid of that restriction on a chorus.

Unrelated people can't do a “chorus”?

You have to be twins to do a “chorus”?

Then let's turn the Gremlin itself into twin Gremlins!

The Blue Witch had said long ago that all Gremlins were related as parents and children, born from an original magic stone.

If there are parent-child relationships, can't there be twin relationships too?

The method for making twin Gremlins was simple.

Carve multiple perfectly identical Moebius rings out of one giant Gremlin.

Carve identical processed pieces out of the same lump.

Then I should be able to call them twins.

There was no particular reason to think processing twin Gremlins this way would succeed.

It might just fail, but I had a feeling it could work. That was why I was doing the experiment.

I wasn't a professor, so I couldn't lay out the theory step by step.

It was the gut instinct of the Wand Maker with the most Gremlin-processing experience in the world. The kind of instinct that told me, This part looks like it'll fit here. This method just feels like it'll work.

I spent nearly a month carving twelve perfectly identical Moebius rings and one Moebius ring a size larger for comparison out of the 80 mm Gremlin.
To achieve a 100% shape match, I took a ridiculously long time, working more slowly than a snail and with extreme care, and concentrated so hard I thought my eyes might bleed.

At one point, I got a nosebleed while concentrating to the absolute limit, and before I knew it, my chin and knees were covered in blood.

But thanks to that, I didn't make a single mistake, and I think the precision was the theoretical maximum I could achieve.

Any error in the processing couldn't possibly have been 1.0 microns or more.

It was a masterpiece. Even real twins wouldn't look this alike.

After finishing the 12+1 Moebius rings, I slept like the dead for three whole days, dragged myself up like a dying turtle, listlessly munched on hardtack, and spaced out for around half a day. Only then could I finally move normally, so I called the Blue Witch over through an eyeball familiar.

The processing was complete. Now all that was left was to test whether they actually worked as expected.

After a while, the Blue Witch came through the Lost Mist. The moment she saw my face at the front door, she asked worriedly,

“Ori, aren't you looking a little worn out?”

“N... No? Probably?”

“You said you'd be concentrating for a while. You did eat, right?”

“I'd be dead if I'd fasted for a month. Enough about that. Come on, get inside. Help me with the experiment.”

I led the Blue Witch into my workshop, showed her the Moebius rings I'd made, and explained the phenomenon I expected.

The Blue Witch tilted her head as she turned one of the twelve perfectly identical Gremlins over in her hand.

“So I hold this, you hold another twin Gremlin, and I join you in a chorus?”

“Yeah. But let's call it ritual magic, not a chorus. I thought about it, and that sounds way cooler, right?”

“Huh? Well, if that's what Ori wants to call it, fine. I understand your prediction that if the two of us perform ritual magic, the magic-power cost will be split in half. But how does the magic come out? Which ring does it come out of? Does it come from between the two rings?”

“Huh? Uh, who knows? I hadn't thought about that at all.”

All I'd thought about was whether ritual magic would work using twin Gremlins. I hadn't considered that part at all.

It's fine, it's fine. We'll find out if we try it, right?

The Blue Witch sighed.

“That's what I mean when I say you don't take danger seriously. What if it misfires? Just in case, let's point our Gremlins in different directions and cast the spell.”

“Okay. Let's do that. We'll use ‘Freeze[ヴアアラー].’ One, two, three, okay? Cast after you say the ‘ee’ in ‘three.’”

The Blue Witch nodded and aimed her Gremlin somewhere it couldn't possibly hit me.

I aimed mine somewhere it couldn't possibly hit the Blue Witch either.

Then we chanted.

“One, two, three! ‘Freeze[ヴアアラー]’!”

The experiment's success was obvious right away.

Both my Moebius ring and the Blue Witch's glowed gold, but only one spell activated.

But that spell shot out of the larger Moebius ring hanging on the wall for the comparison test.

I was stunned.

That one!? I hadn't expected that!

Of all the possible directions, the freezing beam came flying straight at the Blue Witch, so I threw myself in front of her and shielded her with my back.

“Agh!? C-cold! Aah!”

The freezing beam hit me in the chest, and I shivered from the cold.

It was like I'd jumped into an ice bath. I'm gonna freeze, I'm gonna freeze! Cold! So freaking cold!

As I rubbed my goosebumped skin and kicked my feet around, someone grabbed my shoulder hard from behind and forced me to face the Blue Witch.

“Hey! What were you thinking!?”

The Blue Witch was furious. Sh-she's super mad!

I raised both hands and backed away.

“I-I mean, no one could know, no one could predict magic would come out of that ring! Going by the twin theory, that one is probably recognized as the oldest brother among thirteen siblings, so the magic power gathered from the twelve smaller Moebius rings was focused into that slightly larger oldest-brother ring, and—”

I tried to explain my theory and prove my innocence, but the Blue Witch cut me off and shouted at me furiously.

“That's not it! Why did you shield me? What if you died!? I'll kill you! You idiot!”

“No, no, I wasn't trying to shield you! Seriously, not even a bit! The freezing beam came out of nowhere, so it startled me! My body just moved on its own!”

When I hurriedly explained myself, the Blue Witch's voice suddenly trailed off.

“...You shielded me on instinct? Without thinking?”

“Yeah. Sorry, okay!”

That was seriously stupid. If it had hit the Blue Witch, her natural resistance to cold would've made it no big deal.

When I apologized, the Blue Witch went quiet. She didn't move a muscle, so I waved my hand in front of her mask, but there was no response.

“D-dead...!”

“Alive. But going home now.”

“Huh, what about the rest of the experiment?”

“Tomorrow.”

When she finally moved, her speech and movements were weirdly stiff as she headed home.

The ear I caught a glimpse of through the gap between her mask and hair was red too. Maybe she has a cold...?

I wasn't so dense that I actually thought that.

I knew.

The Blue Witch had been embarrassed! Embarrassed enough that her ears turned red!

But about what?

What was there to be embarrassed about? I had no idea.

Well, she was just as weird as I was, so I guess she'd found something to be embarrassed about. Some days are like that.

Anyway, thanks to the Blue Witch's cooperation, I confirmed the experiment had succeeded.

Now all I had to do was coat these 12+1 twin Moebius rings with resin so they wouldn't get scratched and send them to Professor Ohinata.
