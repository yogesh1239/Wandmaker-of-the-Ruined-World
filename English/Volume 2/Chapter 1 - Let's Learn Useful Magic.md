A year and a half earlier, the Shantak Meteor Shower that rained down on Earth had taken electricity from humanity and brought magic in its place. It was what people called the Gremlin Disaster.

Scientific civilization, which had thrived on electricity, collapsed in no time. Monsters, witches, and all kinds of other things started popping up everywhere, and the world became a crucible of chaos.

But as time passed, the chaos melted in that crucible cooled, solidified, and began to take shape.

Humanity had entered the reconstruction phase, riding the chaos scattered everywhere and taking new things from it.

In particular, defeating the giant kaiju and preventing an unprecedented great famine by developing the fertility-magic bypass incantation had come to symbolize reconstruction. I was proud to have given magic wands to the witch and stoat who pulled off both.

I'm an amazing Wand Maker. I'm not being conceited.

I'm so amazing that a dragon kidnapped me and nearly locked me away in its nest as one of its treasures. Doesn't that mean I can call myself a Living National Treasure?

My friend, the Blue Witch, had just saved me from that bad dragon. She helped me haul the stuff she'd recovered from its nest back to Okutama.

As she parked the cart in front of my house and helped me unload, the Blue Witch kept pressing me.

“Hey, won't you reconsider? There's a safer, more comfortable house in Ome. Move to Ome.”

“No. I like my life the way it is. I've got fields to look after too. I'm staying in Okutama forever.”

“What will you do if you get kidnapped again? I taught the Dragon Witch a lesson, but another stray dragon could still show up. Honestly, you were lucky kidnapping was all it did. I might not be able to protect you next time... Please, stay somewhere I can keep an eye on you.”

“No.”

“Tch. Why won't Kei-chan or Ori let me protect them...!?”

The Blue Witch groaned in frustration.

Probably because the things they each want to protect aren't the same? Not that I know.

“Well, it'd be a huge help if you just came by now and then to make sure I'm okay. Don't worry about it so much. You saved me this time by showing up, after all. More importantly, how did you know I was being held prisoner in the dragon's nest?”

“I went to share some apples with you, and your house was wrecked. There were dragon tracks in front of it, and the rice harvest in your paddy had been abandoned halfway through. I understood right away what had happened, so I hurried to that piece of trash's nest.”

“I see.”

Now that she mentions it, there are some pretty obvious clues left behind. The kidnapping scene couldn't be much more blatant.

Good thing the culprit was a Dragon Witch[idiot] without the cunning to hide her crime~!

“I'm worried about you, Ori. You were just kidnapped, and you're already wearing that carefree look. You don't have enough sense of danger. Even if you stay in Okutama, you could move to a house closer to Ome...”

“No, I have a sense of danger too. It's not like I haven't thought of any countermeasures, okay? While I'm fixing the wall of my house, I'm thinking of putting in some steel plates or something. And making a hidden room I can run into. Even if I lose my temper, I won't pick a fight with a dragon next time.”

“You picked a fight with it!? You idiot! I told you to hide!”

The Blue Witch's worry gave way to anger.

Crap. That slipped out.

Well, that part is my fault. I've been careless. But she needs to remember that the Dragon Witch is the one most at fault, and that nobody got hurt in the end (except the Dragon Witch).

After lecturing me for a while about defending myself, the Blue Witch seemed to notice I was only pretending to listen, and she heaved a huge sigh.

“Honestly. You don't understand that these times are so dangerous, no amount of caution is enough. Fine. I get it. I'll come up with security measures on my end. I'm going home today, but lock up properly, and don't go out at night. If you sense danger, don't go near it. Stay put.”

“Roger, Mom. Ah!!! Right, I forgot. Since we're friends now, wanna play a card game sometime? I've got decks at my place. I've only ever played online matches, but I bought physical cards too, so I've got them.”

Playing against the Blue Witch sounds more fun than running a fake match by myself and playing both sides.

I've never once wanted to make friends in my whole life, but having friends seems handy because then I won't have trouble finding opponents for games.

When I called after the Blue Witch to stop her from leaving, she gave me a thoroughly exasperated look.

“More carefree talk... You're not talking about playing cards, are you?”

“No shit. Trading cards. A trading card game. If you don't know how to play, I'll teach you the rules.”

“Fine. See you tomorrow, then.”

The Blue Witch went back to Ome, looking over her shoulder worriedly again and again.

Hmm. Do I really look that oblivious to danger? I can't make sense of it.

I always carry Hendensho so I'm armed, keep a compact first-aid kit with painkillers, disinfectant, and bandages in my pocket, stay away from cliffs in the mountains, and avoid the mountains and rivers the day after rain. I do take precautions, even if it doesn't look like it.

Maybe the Blue Witch and I have different standards for danger: I live peacefully in Okutama, where monsters are far fewer and weaker than elsewhere and there is no messy interpersonal drama, while she's apparently overcome some seriously tragic past.

Thinking about how peaceful the Reiwa era[^1] really had been, I followed her advice: I locked up properly, put a blue tarp over the broken wall as a temporary fix, made a few odds and ends, ate dinner, and turned in early instead of staying up late.

---

The next morning—well past noon.

I was continuing the rice harvest when I received two visitors.

They were the Blue Witch in her usual ragged black robes and mask, and Professor Ohinata in a stylish autumn outfit.

First, I beckoned the Blue Witch over and grilled her in a low voice.

“Are you screwing with me? Why'd you bring her? If there's something you need, letters are fine!”

“So you can learn magic you can use for defense right away. You have trouble learning incantations sent in phonetic symbols, Ori. Learning straight from Kei-chan will be fastest.”

“Quit hitting me with facts.”

I glanced at Professor Ohinata. She held up a basket giving off a warm, mouthwatering smell and flashed me a full-on extrovert smile bright enough to rival the clear autumn sky.

Gah! She's just a kid, yet she's nice enough to remember a gift! But that only makes my stomach hurt more. Talking to Professor Ohinata in human form reminds me of all my own shortcomings, and it stresses me out.

No, she's a good kid. She really is. This isn't about disliking her. We just don't click.

“Oh, right, before I forget. Wait a second.”

Before the impromptu magic lesson that was apparently about to begin squeezed my brain dry, I ducked inside, grabbed a picture frame, and came back.

“Here. I made this picture frame to thank you for saving me yesterday. Use it if you want. Your little sister liked pink, and she had a Java sparrow, right? I worked those into the design.”

I knew the Blue Witch carried a mini album with her little sister's pictures in it (the parts that probably showed her parents and childhood friend had been blacked out. Seriously dark).

The mini album is already pretty beat-up, and moving a picture to the frame and putting it on display seems like a decent option.

When the Blue Witch took the picture frame from me, she didn't move a muscle. Then, just as I started wondering if someone had cast petrification magic on her, she spoke in a faint, trembling voice.

“Thank you.”

“Huh. Are you about to cry right now? Why?”

“Tch! Shut up. If you're going to show consideration, keep it up for at least five seconds.”

“S-Sorry. When I'm talking to the Blue Witch, I let my guard down and my mouth slips. If this is what having a friend I can talk to this easily is like, it's not half bad.”

“...What are you trying to do to my emotions?”

The Blue Witch noticed Professor Ohinata watching our exchange with a huge smile and cleared her throat.

“Anyway, I've explained the situation to Kei-chan. Have her teach you magic to protect yourself. I'll go around Okutama and wipe out the monsters nearby.”

With that, the Blue Witch hurried into the mountains with Cyanos in one hand.

Professor Ohinata, now alone with me, spoke warmly and openly despite how awkward I felt.

“It's been a while, Ori-san. We have been exchanging letters, but I'm happy to see you again like this! I'm happy, but talking to me is still hard for you, isn't it, Ori-san? I picked out magic that seems easy to learn and useful, so let's learn it real quick! The faster you learn it, the faster it'll be over. Let's do our best!”

Professor Ohinata clenched a fist in front of her chest and encouraged me.

Aaaah! Scary! That's the problem: she's organized, takes my feelings into account, and cheerfully supports me! I want her to act more mechanical and businesslike, without a shred of humanity, but I can't reject Professor Ohinata's kindness. That's exactly the problem (I'm losing it).

“Ah, then, let's do this in the workshop... No, the workshop's wrecked right now. Is the living room okay for the lecture?”

“Yes. Excuse me. And here, these are hash browns made with new potatoes. Please have them for dinner!”

After putting the finishing touches on the rice harvest, I brought Professor Ohinata home with me.

Since they'd taste better warm, I snacked on hash browns with hot water while Professor Ohinata gave me a one-on-one lecture.

With a list she'd written on loose-leaf paper in one hand, Professor Ohinata began her assessment.

“Well then.

As for the magic we're going to learn today, your amount of magic power limits the options.

To deal with strong monsters, you need strong magic, and strong magic consumes a lot of magic power. Spells with high magic-power costs are advanced and often contain unpronounceable sounds, though there are exceptions.

Today, let's choose one or two useful spells without those unpronounceable sounds that you can cast with your amount of magic power, Ori-san.

To do that, I want to know how much magic power you have. I've heard you have a lot, Ori-san, but what would that be in terms of the shooting-magic core spell's consecutive-use equivalent?”

“What's a what now?”

When I asked again because I couldn't keep up, Professor Ohinata kindly rephrased it.

“Um, how many times can you use the easiest magic in a row? You know, the spell where you shout `Fire[ア゙ー]` and shoot a white beam, like a screaming beaver.”

“Oh, that one. Hmm, come to think of it, I've never properly counted. How many? Around 50 or 60? I can fire Freezing Javelin[ドウ・ヴアアラー] three times. Four is impossible.”

“Mm-hmm. Then I guess anything above this is impossible... You can just go by instinct, but which of these three would you like to learn: ‘familiar summoning,’ ‘creating Lost Mist,’ or ‘entering suspended animation’?”

“That really leaves two choices, right? Is magic that puts you in suspended animation useful?”

“Monsters, witches, and mages have a harder time detecting you with their five senses or through magic power. We don't know the detailed mechanism, though.”

“That's useful.”

If they call it suspended animation, I figure I won't be able to move, but it seems super handy for hiding until an enemy passes. It feels like weakling magic for getting through things without fighting.

“Still, familiar summoning sounds the most interesting.”

“All right. Then let's learn familiar-summoning magic. Um, the incantation goes like this.”

Professor Ohinata wrote the ominous Japanese sentence “If it meant knowing my son was safe, I'd be willing to gouge out this eye[ヨモホロゲジュヤ・タケタテェエ、クンヌム・ワーア]” in large letters on a blank sheet of loose-leaf paper and showed it to me.

Okay, that's scary.

“Wait a second. Does this magic make you gouge out your own eye?”

“No, you don't gouge anything out. It's okay. The incantation just says that, and when you recite the spell, an eyeball familiar pops out.

This familiar-summoning magic is Eyeball Witch-san's magic. The core word is `Eyeball[クンヌム]`. When you recite it, one eyeball familiar appears, and you can move it freely just by willing it. Besides sharing the familiar's vision, you can hear sounds through it and send your voice through it too. Being able to fly is a big deal too. Its maximum altitude is around 10 m above the ground, though.

The feature of this magic is that once you cast it, you can leave it out until it's destroyed or you make it self-destruct. The user's maximum magic power stays reduced by the amount the spell consumed, and that amount won't recover while the familiar exists. For example, if Ao-san carries the familiar Ori-san summoned, you can communicate and call for help anytime in an emergency.”

“So it's basically a video call.”

What she's saying is nuts. Does that mean I can have a communication device in a world where electricity and communication networks have been destroyed?

Sick! From what I hear, it seems usable for scouting too. I can get as much of an information advantage as I want!

“Exactly. It consumes a lot of magic power, so few humans can use it, but you should be able to manage summoning just one, Ori-san.”

“Does this magic have a maximum communication distance? Like, the signal won't reach past here?”

“Good question. It seems the communication distance depends on your magic-power control skills. Eyeball Witch-san has a communication radius of 180 km, but mine was 20 km when I tried it. I think yours will be about that far too, Ori-san.”

“20 km...”

I pulled out a map while eating hash browns and checked. A 20 km radius covers the distance from Okutama to Ome. That's enough.

“Also, it can carry things up to around 2–3 kg, and while it has no combat function, it can serve as a decoy.”

“So it works as a drone too. I'm learning this one.”

“Huh, you don't want to hear about the other spells? I recommend Lost Mist, though.”

“No, I'm going with the eyeball. It's crazy useful however you look at it.”

The potential to bring back the internet and online shopping with enough of them also leaves a good impression on me. This spell deserves to spread throughout the whole world. That'll be hard when people with little magic power can't use it, though.

“It really is very useful. All right!

Then let's practice right away. Okay, together in a loud voice!

If it meant knowing my son was safe[ヨモホロゲジユヤタケタテエエ]!”

“ヨモホロゲジュヤタケタテタッ... I tripped over it.”

“Are you okay? Hmm, why don't you try pronouncing it slowly at first? `タケタテェエ` is especially easy to get tongue-tied on, so focus on that part.

Okay, slowly, repeat after me! If I could know[タケタテエエ].”

“タケタテーエ. No, that's wrong. タケタテッ, タケタケ... I'm gonna lose it.”

“C-Calm down...!”

With breaks in between, I spent half a day learning the magic for summoning an eyeball familiar.

Professor Ohinata told me during a break that the red magic stone Blood Moon confiscated from the Dragon Witch was, for the time being, in the custody of the Tokyo Witches' Council according to a will the Bloodsucking Mage had left with a lawyer.

Apparently, once magic-language research progressed and ordinary people with high magic power and combat ability had been trained, it would be handed over on the condition that they protect Minato Ward.

It seemed the Bloodsucking Mage had felt there were limits to rule by witches and mages, and had planned to strengthen ordinary people and, by extension, advance their participation in politics.

Smart. If humanity's technology and ability as a whole are brought up, we won't have to let people with power but trash personalities, like the Dragon Witch, run wild.

Rather than giving magic stones to witches and mages who are already strong and making them even stronger, it makes way more sense to give magic stones to ordinary people who have learned magic and give them a leg up.

I only learned about the Bloodsucking Mage after he died, but the more I learn about him, the more his stock goes up in my book.

Meanwhile, the Dragon Witch's stock keeps dropping even when she isn't around, thanks to swiping the magic stone and stuffing it in her pocket against the Bloodsucking Mage's will.

I still think it would've been better to kill her. If that lesson doesn't stick and she tries something again, the Blue Witch will probably go finish her off for good, with no prompting from me.

Anyway, the magic-language research at Ohinata Laboratory, started by the Bloodsucking Mage and carried on by the Foresight Mage, was steadily bearing fruit.

I poked at the softly floating eyeball familiar I'd finally managed to summon after so much pronunciation practice and felt grateful for the unbroken chain of human will and everything it had achieved.

I'm gonna reach in from just outside the circle of human bonds and help myself to those achievements!
